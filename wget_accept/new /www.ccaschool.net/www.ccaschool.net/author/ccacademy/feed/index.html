In May of 2014 a committee was formed to look closely at our safety and security at CCA. As a result of the work of this
committee, a new PA system was installed and procedures for our lockdown and lockouts were improved, and outside door
handles and locks were replaced. A large part of the committee’s work was to explore the possibility of our own employees
carrying concealed guns with the purpose of acting as school security officers, in addition to their regular duties. Members of
the committee researched the steps that other districts have taken, and we found their advice, as well as the input from our
attorney and insurance company very useful.

Our next step was to elicit the input from our most important voices, our CCA families. We had a great turn-out at our CCA
community discussion back in July, and since then we have been overwhelmed by the support of our families. THANK YOU!!

Some of our employees who were interested in acting as a Cardinal Security Personnel (CSP) began training in the late
summer months. Each participant spent a minimum of 25 hours in classroom training and range time, and another 11 hours in
an active shooter response training at CCA. In addition, all CSP passed the POST qualification, which is the same qualification
that is required from our local law enforcement. Finally, at the December 3rd board meeting, the participants were approved.
We are excited to finally be able to take this step to make CCA a safer place. Once all documentation is submitted to our
insurance company, our CSP’s can begin carrying, which will likely occur after returning from Christmas break.
