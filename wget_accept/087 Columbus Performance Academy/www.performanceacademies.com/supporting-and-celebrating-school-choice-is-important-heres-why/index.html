Supporting (and Celebrating) School Choice is Important; Here’s Why

11-12-2013 | Myrrha Satow

Yesterday, the Huffington Post posted “An Open Letter To National School Choice Week”. In the letter, Steve Nelson barrages School Choice Week officials with wild accusations, including accelerating the “demise of public education in America”. He hurls further outrageous insults like the groups that fund School Choice Week “hope to shrink government to a size where it might be drowned in the bathtub”, and further posits, “Do you wish the same for public education?” He goes on to erroneously charge school choice with “promoting segregation”, funneling money to “fundamentalist religious schools through contrived mechanisms” and other equally paranoid claims, such as the big kahuna: School choice “violates the First Amendment”! Wow. Just wow. Mr. Nelson, I don’t believe those of us who have supported school choice efforts for nearly twenty years and who have made it our life’s work actually wake up every morning conceiving and contriving ways to “violate the First Amendment”, promote segregation, or “drown the government”. In sum, this is just crazy talk.

School Choice embodies the provisions of the First Amendment, free exercise of religion, free speech, and free press (so yes, you can make these false accusations without recourse), and so on. What more fundamental right should a parent and child have than the right to a free quality education of THEIR choice? What more fundamental right is there, I ask you? Whether a parent seeks smaller class sizes, a safe school, a positive learning environment, a unique learning experience tailored to their child’s learning style, better communication from their child’s school, or better academics than what their local public school offers, shouldn’t they be able to choose that for their child? Education is NOT a one size fits all. It simply is not. Can you in your heart deny a parent the right to choose what they think is best for their child? We have parents in our schools, for example who have multiple children, and they may send one to us and one to the local public school and one to a private school to meet their children’s unique needs. Do we begrudge that family for making the best decision for the child who attends a public school? Absolutely not. We embrace it. We embrace it because, yes, we actually believe in this thing called “school choice”. Fundamentally, we should respect parents for knowing their own children better than anyone else.

By the way, I agree with you that schools should be equitably funded. When charter schools are funded at anything close to the same levels as traditional public school districts, it would be a great day indeed for students who attend charter schools. In the meantime, we will continue doing more with far less money per student than the districts receive. We are proud that many charters perform as well or better at less cost per student for taxpayers.

Shame on Huffington Post for posting this letter that is fraught with false claims and sensationalism that further confuses parents, keeps the “edufighting” going, and seeks to deny parents the right to choose the best school option for their child. This is just plain bad for kids. The children we serve are ALL our children. School choice is for all–traditional public, private, homeschool, and charter, because one size does not fit all.
