Dress Code
Eagle Academy Elementary is committed to helping students develop the skills to be successful in college and careers.  An important aspect of this is appearance.  From job interviews to work uniforms, how an individual looks presents an impression either positively or negatively.  Our dress code is designed to present a positive impression and create an environment where learning is clearly the focus.
Purpose of Eagle Academy Dress Code:
1.      Provide positive and productive learning environment
2.      Protect student health and safety
3.      Promote appropriate academic behavior and respect
4.      Prepare students for college and career expectations

Elementary (Grades K-5)
Students are expected to be in uniform during each school day. Students found to be in violation of the dress code policy will have a parent called; a parent will then need to bring their child appropriate clothing. Alternately, Eagle Academy reserves the right to provide the student with appropriate clothing for the day. Repeated violations of the Dress Code Policy will result in disciplinary action per the Student Code of Conduct. On field trip days, students not in proper uniform will NOT be permitted to travel. Exceptions to this rule will be indicated on the permission slip that is sent home to be signed prior to the trip.

Tops
* Any color SOLID polo shirt. Each student will receive 2 Eagle Academy logo shirts FREE
* Black or white long sleeve or turtleneck shirts may be worn underneath
* Black, navy, white or khaki sweater; no hooded jackets or sweatshirts
*  Shirts must overlap the bottom (tucked in or out); school reserves the right to request that shirts be tucked in based on student appearance.

Bottoms
* Black, navy, or khaki dress pants, jumper, skirt or shorts. Shorts are permitted to be worn when temperatures warrant shorts.
* Shorts and skirts must be long enough that they pass the fingertips when hands are held to side.
* Undergarments or shorts worn under pants should not be seen
* Tights, stockings or ankle length leggings may be worn under skirts, jumpers, or shorts.
* Belts are required for any pants, shorts, or skirts that are unable to stay on the waist without constant adjustment.

Shoes
* Shoes must be closed toe, closed back and have a hard bottom. Athletic shoes are the best choice for Elementary students.
* Sandals, house shoes, winter boots, or high heels that are judged by Eagle Academy Elementary staff to be unsafe or disruptive to the learning environment will not be permitted.

Accessories
Permitted:
* no more than 2 pairs of earrings
* one ring per hand
* one necklace
* 2 bracelets

Jewelry with pointed spikes will not be permitted. Hats and other head coverings are not permitted except for documented religious purposes. No hairstyle, cut, or color should cause distraction. Heavy chains for wallets, belts or as jewelry will not be permitted. Sunglasses will not be permitted except for documented medical reasons. Students may wear modest jewelry and other accessories in moderation. Any accessory that staff deems is a distraction to classroom functions will need to be removed.  Facial piercings may not be worn at school.

Dress Down Days      
On special occasions, students will be given a “Dress Down Day”.  Typically, jeans and t-shirts are permitted on those days only.  No short shorts or open toe shoes. Parents will receive a written note home the day before a dress down day.

Secondary (Grades 6-11)
Tops    
* Any color polo shirt (sweater, sweatshirt or turtle neck may be worn in colder weather)
* Shirts must overlap the bottom; administration reserves the right to request that shirts be tucked in based on student appearance.

Bottoms
*  Black, navy or khaki dress pants, jumper, skirt or shorts
* Shorts and skirts must be long enough that they pass the fingertips when hands are held to side.
* Undergarments or shorts worn under pants should not be seen
* Tights, stockings or ankle length leggings may be worn under skirts, jumpers or shorts
* Belts are required for any pants, shorts or skirts that are unable to stay on the waist

Accessories
* Hats and other head coverings are not permitted except for documented religious purposes.
* Sunglasses will not be permitted except for documented medical reasons.
* Jewelry or other accessories deemed by administration to be unsafe for the student or peers will not be permitted.
* Gang-related apparel is not permitted. Bandanas, do-rags, handkerchiefs are not permitted. Any apparel judged by administration to be gang-related will require the student to change or remove clothing. Gang-related apparel will not be accepted in the classroom.

Shoes:
* Shoes must be clean and in good repair
* Flip-flops, house shoes, slippers, winter boots, high heels or any shoe that is judged to be unsafe by administration will not be permitted
*  Bare or stocking feet are unsafe and prohibited.

Dress Down Days       
On special occasions, students will be given a “Dress Down Day”.  Typically, jeans and t-shirts are permitted on those days only.  No short shorts or open toe shoes. Parents will receive a written note home the day before a dress down day.
