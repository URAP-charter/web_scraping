Attendance
The following policies are based on the Ohio Revised Code, chapter 3321 School Attendance.

Daily attendance is expected for students to reach their full academic potential.  Additionally, students are required by law to attend school with few, documented exceptions.  Overall, parents are required by law to ensure that their children maintain a good attendance record.

After 8:00 a.m., students will be marked absent.  It is the parent’s responsibility to call the school at 419-691-4876 by 9:00 a.m. to report a child absent.

When a student is late to school, the student must report to the school office to get a late pass before going to class.  Parents are required to call the school office to inform the school of the child’s late arrival.  Excessive tardies may be reported as truancy.

   After any absence, a student MUST bring a signed note to school.  The student should present his/her note signed by his/her parent or guardian stating the date(s) of absence, the reason for the absence, and a current phone number.  This note is to be presented to the child’s teacher.  The school will then place a call to confirm the absence excuse.

Excused Absences
* Death in the family
* Family illness (an emergency situation requiring the student to be absent from school)
* Quarantine of the home by Health Officials
* Head lice (one day only)
* Personal illness with doctor’s note
* Long term personal illness (with proper documentation)
* College visits (pre-approved by office)
* Documented court or court ordered activities
Unexcused Absences
* Overslept
* Doctor’s Appointment (all day)
* Chronic head lice
* Lack of transportation/missed the bus
* Vacations
* "Not feeling well” (no note from doctor)
Absences counted as present
* Religious observations (with prior written request)
* Illness requiring hospitalization
* Doctor/dental appointment not exceeding 2 hours with prior notification given to the office

Students, who return to school without a note, or with a note missing a signature or current phone number, will have the absence coded as unexcused.

After three (3) consecutive days of absence, a student must have a note from a medical doctor or the absences will be coded as truancy.  However, a period of extended illness (i.e. Chicken pox) may be considered a single absence for this requirement. All absences documented with medical verification notes will be excused.

After five (5) unexcused absences, parents should expect to receive a warning letter concerning truancy laws.  Parents with a child with a chronic illness that results in frequent absences may have a medical doctor complete a form to keep on file at school.  After review by school personnel, the form may exempt the parent/student from securing a doctor’s statement each time the student has a related absence for that school year.

Attendance reports are sent home at the end of each quarter. It is the responsibility of the school to report all attendance concerns to the Juvenile Division of the Lucas County Prosecutor’s Office. Students who have greater than 10 unexcused absences and/or tardies may be reported to the Juvenile Courts. Students who are habitually truant are at risk of being retained.

Withdrawing from Eagle Academy

In order to withdraw, a parent must fill out a withdraw form and returned to the school office. Failure to send a child to school without office notification for 10 consecutive days will trigger review and possible notification of juvenile services.

Early Dismissal

While we understand that situations may arise, your student’s attendance throughout the school day is very important.  If you need to pick up your child early, please inform the office early in the day. Parents are asked to refrain from habitually picking their child(ren) up early from class, as this results in disruption of the classrooms.

Make up work

Make up work will only be given for excused absences. It is the responsibility of the students or parent/guardian to contact the school to obtain make up assignments for excused absences.   Students who know when they will be absent should obtain assignments from their teachers prior to being absent.  All absences will be considered by teachers in determining the participation portion of student grades.  Students may receive full credit for work missed during an excused absence as long as the work is completed properly and submitted on time.  Students will have equivalent to the number of days of the excused absence to make up missed work.

 It is up to the discretion of the teacher to accept or refuse work for unexcused absences.
