  1. Introductions

 

  1. Principals Report

 

Family Night Oct 8th

$5 per family at the door. Proceeds go to Adopt-A-Family. Event to be held 7-8pm. Inquired whether to do the baked goods again. We did make a profit, however at the end of the night prices were being reduced drastically to not have left over items.

 

Outdoor Education May 18-20th.

We will be at Heartland this year. It is a 6th grade trip that allows for a smoother transition to junior high and an opportunity for the students to meet with 7th grade faculty.

 

Watch Dog Dads

Costs are $300 for start-up materials. The kick off consists of “Doughnuts with Dad”. The Dads will wear identifying t-shirts and play a video from the Today Show. The Dads will be in the building Monday and Friday for either a half day or a whole day and present at recess and small groups. The Dads will need to pass a Level 1 Background Check only, as staff will always be present. No Dad will be left alone with the students. This is a great opportunity for those without a male role model to interact, as well as giving the boys a chance to ask questions in our predominately female staffed building. Kara will be partner up with Becky Hornberger at Violet. It was suggested that a sign-up sheet be placed out during Family Night to peak interest. We will ask all prior WDDs to wear their shirts and be available to talk with interested Dads. Kim Nevers will send a ballot for e-mail vote, as not enough voting members are present.

 

Million Word Club

Jamie Watts, our ELA coach, would like funds to purchase shirts. Each student reaching a million words read would receive a shirt. It’s estimated the shirts will cost $7. Kim Nevers will research pricing options based on line item budget. Harmon passes out approximately 100 shirts. We expect our student body to achieve higher number due to Gateway and larger attendance.

 

6th Grade Band

Band is seeking a $75 donation for incentives for 6th Grade band members. This is already something in our line items but does need approved again. Kim Nevers will send an e-mail ballot.

 

Parent Orientation

Kara would like feedback. It was suggested that the introductions of the staff be made in the individual classrooms versus meeting in the gym shortly prior and heading back to classrooms. The overall consensus was that the meetings felt rushed. The announcement for 5 minute warning came too early and was too long. Some felt it was too late after school had started and would like meeting prior to classes resuming or being in full swing. The parking was much more manageable with the smaller groups than when the entire school met at once.

Conferences

Notification and sign up will be sent out. The conferences will be held towards the end of October.

 

Coffee with a Counselor

Kelly will be doing these with parents. A one call message will go out in October. The meetings will be held during the weekdays.

 

  1. Treasurer Report

 

Not present, however no exchange of money has taken place yet this school year.

 

  1. Old Business

 

Bicentennial Festival/Carnival Games

The event was free except PTO games in the PTO Fun Zone and food vendors. Six out of 14 PTOs participated. Target donated money and payroll. Meijer donated money for candy. Another group donated two bicycles. The PTOs bought color changing cups. We made $338 for our PTO. We have 390 cups left. It was agreed that we’d sell the remaining cups for $1 at Family Fun Night. $1 was the agreed upon price by all PTO’s involved should the cups be sold at school.

 

Membership

We are waiting on the list to e-mail for sign ups.

 

  1. New Business

 

PEF Basket

The Pink Princess tree is due October 15th. We will be getting a donation from Precision Jewelers in either the form of a gift certificate or piece of jewelry not to exceed $200. Seeking other ideas for gifts that go along with the theme. J. Nicole Spa or any other local businesses that women like to frequent to be pampered.

 

Meet the School Board Candidates Night

October 14th from 6-8pm. There are four people running for two seats. Three have already RSVP’d to the event. There is one incumbent and three new running for the positions. We do have a moderator. The open event will be held at Heritage as well as available on-line.

 

  1. Programs

AR Store

Sign up will go out after bookfair for the October 23rd shop. We’ve decided to put into place a “use it or lose it” for students. Too many were hoarding points until the end of the year for the few giftcards.

BookFair Oct 12-21st Friendly Monster Theme

Sheet went home Wednesday. The set-up is October 12th. Kim Jenkins is making friendly monsters out of milk jugs. Should we have one each class contribute a monster, assign it to the Craft Club or make a competition? The winning classroom will get popsicles, which are already in the PTOs possession, and a spirit stick. The teacher will receive a $10 gift card. Voting will be held during lunch periods. The winning monster will be the money holder for the suckers. The sucker money raised will be used to buy wish list books for teachers. Monsters are due by October 9th. Turn in clean, empty milk jugs to the office.

Grandparent Luncheon

A sign-up genius will be sent out, as the PTO staffs this event. There will be two classroom per lunch. This year we will be combining homeroom teams versus having them on alternating weeks. A Flyer went home that needs to be corrected to remove Davies/Ramsdale from this group. A notice will go out to inform those students.

Box Tops Contest

Should we repeat the contest from last year? And idea from Heritage is that a water balloon toss match may work better. We also will seek out a way to be better prepared for filling the water balloons. Other ideas include taping Kara/Kelly to the wall. The Goal is 1500 box tops to participate.

Fall Fundraiser

Many have not turned in their Walk-A-Thon money. There is less money this year than last. An extra recess will be offered as an incentive for the classes that can raise $350.

Spirit Wear

There is a meeting planned with Tom on September 28th.

Leadership Teams

These groups meet once a month. There are 17 in total. Students were able to express interest in their top three and assigned by staff based on availability.

Staff Appreciation

Yearbook Club

We are determining the role the students will play in the Club. We also need to decide if we are using Mike from Fairfield Photography or looking into Interstate Studios. We may also be able to add Yearbook to one of the available Leadership teams. We are operating without any student cameras at this point. It is in our best interest to order less and possibly raise the retail, so as not to lose money this year. Kim Nevers and Kara will contact Sarah Diehl.

Talent Show
