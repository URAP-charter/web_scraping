Courses and Class Registration

Suggestions Before Requesting Courses Through Infinite Campus

Print out a scheduling transcript to view previous courses, grades, and credits earned by logging into Infinite Campus portal account through a web browser, then going to “Reports” and “Scheduling Transcript.”

View webcast of counselor’s presentations given to students.

Review the Course Registration Guide (under “Junior High School Course Guide and Fees”) to learn more about the classes, credits, fees, etc.

Review the other helpful documents on the Course Registration page to learn more about the graduation requirements, grading policies, diplomas, NCAA standards, etc.

Fill out a Course Request worksheet for your grade level to assist you once you’ve logged into the portal (these forms do not need to be turned in). Worksheets were distributed by counselors, and are also available on the Course Registration page

Log into the portal using a web browser (Safari, Firefox, Chrome, Internet Explorer). Mobile devices using a web browser/internet connection can be used, but you will not be able to use the mobile app for course registration.

High School Registration - February 8 - February 26 at 2:00pm

Dear Parents,

It is time for our 8th grade students to register for Pickerington High School North! This is an exciting time for our students, but we also know that many students are nervous about the transition to high school. On Monday, February 8 and Tuesday, February 9, Mr. Peirano, one of the counselors from Pickerington High School North, will be visiting our 8th grade social studies classes.

To prepare for next week’s visit, please take some time this weekend to review the High School Course Guide with your child. The High School Course Guide is online at http://www.pickerington.k12.oh.us/pickerington-north-high/courses-and-class-registration/. Mr. Peirano and I will be reviewing the course guide, discussing graduation requirements and the new schedule next week with students in Social Studies classes. If time allows, we will have students log on to their Infinite Campus account and register for classes.

Registration will be open and active from Monday, February 8 – Friday, February 26 at 2:00pm. Registration must happen on a computer or laptop; you will not be able to register on phones or iPads. More information on how to register will be sent home with your child when we visit their social studies class.

There will be a College Credit Plus meeting at Pickerington High School Central on February 17 at 7:00pm. Please plan to attend this meeting if you are interested in having your child earn college credit while they are in high school.

Please note that when your student is at the high school, they will be assigned to a school counselor by their last name. At this time, our high school counselors are available through the registration process via email only.

Junior High Presentation for 2016-17 school year

Summer 2016 Credit Recovery / New Credit Program

Pickerington Schools will offer a credit recovery/new credit program during the summer of 2016. The program is open to any current high school or past students of the Pickerington Schools. Out-of-district students may be considered if space is available.

Begin the registration process by filling out this online form.

Credit Recovery is a self-guided, computer-based program that will allow a student to earn credits in areas where the student has previously been unsuccessful.

New Credit courses are only available to students on an approved early graduation plan or with approval of the Director of Secondary Instruction.

Courses will vary in terms of the hourly requirements needed to complete a course. Each course will be worth ½ to 1 (0.5 – 1.0) Carnegie units. There are ways for a student to accelerate progress by pre-testing out of some units. Students will be able to take as many credits as they want during the session but will not be permitted to work on more than one course at a time.  

Registration will be on a first-come basis, although students who should have graduated in June 2016 will be given the first priority of placement. All Pickerington students will be given priority before the placement of any student from outside the school district.

Summer Session

  • June 7 through July 8 at Pickerington High School North
  • Both the student and parent must attend an orientation from 6 p.m. or 7 p.m. May 26 at Pickerington High School North Lecture Hall.
  • If necessary, appointments will be available for orientation by calling the Teaching and Learning Department at 614.920.6167.

Class hours

  • Regular Session: Tuesday through Friday; 10 a.m. – 2 p.m., June 7 – July 8
  • Extended Session: Tuesday through Friday; 10 a.m. – 2 p.m., July 13 – July 22

Students taking courses for credit recovery must attend school during class hours until the course is completed. If they do not, there will be no extensions.

All tests must be completed in person during class hours

Cost  

The cost is $200 for in-district students during regular session. This cost covers all courses completed during regular session. An additional $200 will be collected for each course taken during the extended session

The cost is $250 per course for out-of-district students.

Registration and payment to be completed online

Questions should be directed to the Teaching and Learning Department at 614.920.6167 or email: juanita_lemke@plsd.us    

Summer Physical Education Classes

Pickerington Schools will offer a physical education course this summer for 1/4 credit. The class is open to students who will be in grades 8 -12  for the 2016 -17 school year. For next year’s eighth graders to be eligible, they must have:

  • successfully completed junior high physical education with a “B” grade or better, and
  • meet grade 9 literacy requirements.

This course will be at Ridgeview STEM Junior High School beginning Friday, May 27, through Thursday, June 9, 2016. There will be no class on Memorial Day, May 30. Students may miss one day; however, that day must be made up on Monday, June 13, to receive credit.

The cost for this course is $175. Registration and payment will be done online. Click here for more information, registration and instructions for payment is available.
