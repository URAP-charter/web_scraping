Human Resources

The Human Resources Department supports the education program through recruitment and selection processes of highly qualified educators and support staff to fill vacant teaching, support and administrative positions. In addition, the department is responsible for conducting orientations for new employees, assisting staff with license requirements and maintenance of Individual Professional Development Plans.

As an equal opportunity employer, it is the policy of the Pickerington Local School District that no candidate for a position in the District shall be discriminated against on the basis of race, creed, sex, color, national origin, religion, age, sexual orientation, disability, citizenship status, political affiliation, marital status or other human differences. The district has a policy of active recruitment of qualified minority candidates.

The Office of Assessment and Accountability

The Office of Assessment and Accountability works with teachers and administration in the district to ensure information and best practices are discussed and shared across the 14 school buildings. The office oversees the development and revision of job descriptions and the completion of all evaluations for all certificated employees. Additionally, the office helps to provide professional growth opportunities for all Pickerington Schools employees.

In addition, this department works with local colleges and universities to improve the experience for interns in the district as part of the field placement and/or student teaching requirements.

Apply to Work in Pickerington Schools
All applications for positions within Pickerington Schools are completed through Applitrack.
Thank you for your interest in the Pickerington Local School District. We are proud that our schools and personnel are well regarded in the central Ohio area. We are looking for highly qualified professional educators and support staff who wish to be part of a system that is noted for excellence.
More information about the district is available in our recruitment packet.
As an equal opportunity employer, it is the policy of the Pickerington Local School District that no candidate for a position in the District shall be discriminated against on the basis of race, creed, sex, color, national origin, religion, age, sexual orientation, disability, citizenship status, political affiliation, marital status or other human differences. The district has a policy of active recruitment of qualified minority candidates.

The Pickerington Local School District uses an on-line application process for all positions.

  • Please follow the directions carefully, and complete as much of the application as possible.
  • Please do not submit additional information and/or copies to the Human Resources Department.

These items may be electronically sent via the on-line application process or if called for an interview, brought in at that time.

Open positions are first posted internally for current staff members. Positions not filled through this process are then noted as openings as part of the on-line application process.

Submitting an application on-line requires access via computer to the internet. Should you not have access to a computer, you might want to use one at your local library.

When we have a vacancy in your interest area we will screen applications on file and contact selected candidates to begin the interview process.

Applications are normally kept for only 18 months. You may update your on-line application with additional information at any time via the Internet.

Should you be contacted for an interview and require any special accommodations, please contact the Human Resources Department at 614-833-2110. This information will be used only in a manner consistent with the Americans With Disabilities Act of 1990, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 and Chapter 4112 of the Ohio Revised Code.

Thank you again for your interest in our fine school system and best wishes as you pursue your career.

 

Substitute Teachers and Administrators

Persons wishing to substitute for Pickerington Schools should contact Bobbi Williams at the Fairfield County Educational Service Center, 740.653.3193, to begin the application and approval process.

The Educational Service Center will forward approved names to our district for approval by the Board of Education.

Substitute pay is subject to all applicable federal, state and local taxes. Additional deductions will be taken for State Teachers Retirement System contributions and Medicare tax.

Those approved by the Board of Education will be set up in the Automated Substitute Placement & Absence Management (AESOP) system.

Substitutes should dress in a style that reflects good grooming and professionalism.

Effective Oct. 6, 2016, substitute teacher daily rate of pay is $105, pro-rated for half-day assignments. Starting with the 21st day of subbing (based on a full work day) during a given school year, the substitute rate is increased be $115.

Substitutes assigned to one specific teaching position shall, after 60 consecutive days (long-term), will be placed on the teacher’s salary schedule at the BA-0 salary level.

The pay rate for retired substitute teachers will be at $100 per day. Starting with the 21st day, the rate will be $110 (based on a full work day).

Payment on the teacher’s salary schedule for long-term assignments will not be retro-active to the first day of substituting.

Substitute teachers and substitute administrators are paid on the 5th and 20th of each month, pending submission of days worked.

Substitute teachers and substitute administrators will be contacted by the district’s AESOP manager if his/her Child Abuse Reporting document needs to be updated for record purposes.

Substitute placement for the school district does not imply permanent employment with the district.

Substitutes are used on an “as-needed” basis, and implies no guarantee to any specific number of days of substituting. The school district reserves the right to determine its specific needs for substitute employees.
