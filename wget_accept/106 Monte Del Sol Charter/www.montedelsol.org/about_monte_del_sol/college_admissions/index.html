College Acceptances

The Path to Graduation & College Acceptances 

We have developed a strong and successful strategy to guide students towards Graduation and beyond beginning at the final 8th Grade Parent Conference during which Advisors go over the High School Four Year Plan.  Throughout Freshman and Sophomore years Advisors monitor each student's progress to help them stay on track. 

In 11th Grade students take the PSAT test in the Fall and in the second semester we use test scores and five semester's worth of grades to help us guide each individual student, set goals and take corrective measures as necessary.  

Also during the Junior year parents and students attend College Nights where we provide information on all aspects of the college application process, (considering options, research, visiting colleges, testing, completing applications, financial aid and merit scholarships). The College Guidance Counselor meets with all Juniors and their families in order to determine the individual Senior year requirements, college and career goals.   

In Senior Year, students meet with college representatives and begin work on college applications and essays. The Guidance Counselor meets with students to discuss appropriate college choices, proofread applications and edit essays.  We encourage all students to meet with the Guidance Counselor as often as necessary to refine the decision-making process.  

Approximately 92% of Monte del Sol's graduates attend college--about 20% percent begin their higher education at Santa Fe Community College and 70% go to four-year colleges.  The remaining 10% choose to take either a 'gap' year or to begin working right away.
