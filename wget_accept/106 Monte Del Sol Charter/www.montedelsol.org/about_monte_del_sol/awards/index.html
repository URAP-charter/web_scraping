Awards and Accomplishments

We invite you to take a little time to read and celebrate our students' successes, current and past.  

AWARDS TO STUDENTS 

Meghan Hill and Katelynn James (Class of 2015) won First Place in the 2015 New Mexico Supercomputing Challenge gleaning multiple trophies, college scholarships, and an internship at Los Alamos National Lab for Meghan. 

Bailey Colfax (Class of 2015) is a semi-finalist for the 2015 National Merit Scholarship.  

Meghan Hill (Class of 2015) won the Yale Science and Engineering Association Award and was chosen to attend the 2014 International Science and Engineering Fair in Los Angeles. 

Rosemary Elliott-Smith (Class of 2014) was a finalist for the 2014 National Merit Scholarship. Rosemary has also earned a paid summer 2014 internship at The Smithsonian Institute, Washington DC, in the field of paleontology. 

Jacob Romero (2014) and Rosemary Elliott-Smith were recognized by the National Hispanic Recognition Program. 

Silowen Zeir (Class of 2013) won the Los Alamos Foundation LANL Employees' Bronze Scholarship  

Henry 'Hank' Wikle (Class of 2012) was a finalist for the 2012 National Merit Scholarship. He won the 2012 Los Alamos Science Award

Zachary Graybill (2012) and Rosemary Elliott-Smith won The Santa Fe Institute / Santa Fe Alliance for Science Award for excellence in scientific inquiry.  

The Pamela A. White Award is presented to a young woman upon Graduation who has shown a commitment to diversity and anti-oppression work. Recipients from 2007 to present are: Victoria Weiner, Dolna Smithback, Elizabeth McCann, Fabiola Perez, Tenzin Dheden, Mexica Calderon, Sedona Walker-Morrissey and Awa Faridah Ndiaye.   

The Frye Family Foundation Leadership Award is awarded to students who demonstrate leadership in making the world a better place and dedication to acdemic self-improvement in spite of obstacles.  Recipients to date (in alphabetical order): Melissa Cruz, Valeria Cruz, Tenzin Dheden, Nicholas Garcia, Rafael Garcia, Juan Heras, Denise Loya, Monique Lujan, Faridah Ndiaye, Fabiola Perez, Andrew Peterson, Meilani Pruitt, Taylor Schneider, Kelly Smith, Alexandria Solano, Matthew Ritchie, Adrianna Tapia, Jake Theis, Sebastian Weissbarth.    

The Rose Simmons Memorial Scholarship honors and carries forward 15-year-old Rose's visionary environmental leadership.  Recipients to date: Faridah Ndiaye, Mildred Rogriguez and Emma Zuckerman who attended the Bioneers Conference in San Raphael, CA (2013), and Aram Brown, Mexica Calderon and Kyle Miller who participated in the Findhorn Foundation Sustainability Week for Youth in Scotland (2011 and 2012). 

In Spring 2009, 10th Grade Student, Colin O'Hara, received the Jon Smeltzer Memorial Award for Best Delegate and Best Diplomat by The Council of International Relations for his work with Model United Nations.

Jamillah Abdullah (Class of 2009) received a Bill and Melinda Gates Scholarship. 

In 2004 and 2005, The Ronald M. Simon Family Foundation awarded high school and college scholarships totaling over $300,000 to Monte del Sol students, Anarosa Torres-Cruz, Cesar Sanchez Guardado, Tsetan Tenzin Gyaltsen, Pilar Nieto, Yadira Martinez, Janet Romo, Isaiah Nash, Raymi Reynolds Lynch, Hector Sanchez, Daniel Loya and Tenzin Nyima Lhamo. 

In 2004, two eleventh graders were accepted early to colleges with sizeable scholarships:  Annie Kohn to Cornish School of the Arts in Seattle and Kalu Long to the United World College in Las Vegas, NM.

AWARDS TO FACULTY

Rhonda Ward won the 2015 Teacher of The Year Appreciation by the Supercomputing Challenge Board. 

Language Arts teacher, Alfredo Celedón Luján, has been awarded the 2015 'Golden Apple' New Mexico Teacher of the Year.   

In 2007, Visual Arts teacher, Nancy Sue Michels, received the Partners in Educaton Teachers who Inspire Award.

In 2006,  History teacher, Liso Otero, received the Partners in Education Teachers who Inspire Award.  

In March 2005, Choir, History and Government teacher, Ms. Terry Wyrick, was awarded the 'Golden Apple' New Mexico Teacher of The Year.

In 2004, Founding Principal, Tony Gerlicz, was awarded a scholarship for charter school leaders to attend a course at the Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University.

INTERNATIONAL 

Our World Language teachers have taken students to Oaxaca, Mexico five times including for summer programs; to Japan six times, and to England, France and Spain four times.  Plus, Monte students have participated in special trips to Russia, Belgium, India, Scotland and Bali. In May 2014 more than thirty students will travel to Costa Rica and stay with host families.  

Since the founding of the school we have hosted exchange students from Mexico, Japan, Germany, France, Spain, Denmark, Thailand and China;

Over the past eight summers, twenty Monte del Sol students have participated in the Bali Art Project in Bali, Indonesia. 

In 2008, English teacher, Andrea Cermanski, spent the Fall Semester in Kolkata, India on a Fulbright Teacher Exchange Scholarship.  Her Indian counterpart, Sohini Das, joined us for that semester and won our hearts.  

Inspired by Ms. Das, five students and a teacher spent a month in India in the summer of 2009. 

Three of our students have traveled with the Sustainable Global Leadership Alliance's service program worldwide. 

In 2010, 11th Grader, Gabriel Rima, flew to Haiti to help with earthquake relief.

Since its inception, Monte del Sol has developed sister school relationships in Oaxaca, Tsuyama, Japan, Nantes, France, and Holguin, Cuba 

SCHOOL-WIDE AWARDS 

Since 2000, our Mentorship Program has matched over seventeen hundred students to over 850 volunteer mentors from all professional arenas in Santa Fe.   In 2004, the Mentorship Program received the National Civic Star Award from the American Association of School Administrators.

In September 2010, former Head Learner, Angela Ritchie, was invited to speak at the Gates' Foundation conference on Best Cooperative Practices between Charter and Traditional Public Schools' Conference.   

The Foundation for Monte del Sol has been the recipient of five Frye Family Scholarships for each of the last eight years. 

In 2004, we received a competitive Federal Charter School Dissemination Grant to share best practices and partner with the Walatowa Charter School on the Jemez Pueblo. 
 
ARTS  

Student art exhibits are hosted by Santa Fe's premier art venues including the Georgia O'Keeffe Museum's Education Annex, The Center for Contemporary Arts and Santa Fe University of Art and Design.  Please go to our Arts page for even more awards and accolades for our student artists. 

Student films have won awards in youth film festivals around the state including the Desert Light film competition;

In 2004, two students returned with awards from a Spoken Word competition in Los Angeles and in 2003 our students won First Place in the statewide Jimmy Santiago Baca Poetry Slam;

Three students received '04-'05 All State Choir Designations, a high honor for a small school;

ATHLETICS 

Our Boys' Varsity Soccer team were seeded 4th this year in the State Championships and advanced to the semi-finals. The best run so far in Dragon soccer history.  Go Dragons !

Also in Soccer we've had District Coach of The Year (Mazatl Galindo) and District Players of The Year (Matt McChesney, Luis Lozoya) three years in a row (2011-2013).    

This season, our girls' Volleyball program had the most successful season to date with three District wins.  Way to go Lady Dragons!

Boys' Baseball has qualified for State two years in a row.  

Our Basketball District wins date back to 2003.  

Our Girls' Track team still holds the State 4 x 100m record.  

SUSTAINABILITY  

In 2006, we received a $208,000 grant from the State of New Mexico to install a 75,000 gallon water catchment tank; 25,000 of those gallons to be used for sewage treatment and subsequent irrigation;

Our student-designed and built water catchment and greenhouse gained the attention of the Department of Energy, Minerals and Natural Resources as a pilot project in greening an existing school building;

In Fall 2010, we harvested over 50 lbs of organically-grown vegetables each week, all of which were prepared for school lunches. 

In Summer 2009, we created a Memorial Garden to honor the lives of 10th grader, Rose Simmons and 11th grader, Julian Martinez. The Memorial Garden also honors the lives of Hoku Donovan Smith, Isaiah Nash, Skye Seppanan, Brandon Copelin-Brown and Chris Calleweart.
