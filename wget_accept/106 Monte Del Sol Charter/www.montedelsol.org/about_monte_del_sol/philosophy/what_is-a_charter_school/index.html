What is a Charter School?

A Charter School is an independent public school of choice with its own governing board to make decisions and set policy. This gives charter schools greater freedom to design and develop an innovative curriculum and utilize different teaching styles and methods as long as they meet state-mandated content and performance standards. Charter schools receive their operating funds based on the number of students enrolled, just like other public schools, but are free to set and manage their own budget, create their own unique school culture, hire and fire their own staff, design their instructional program, develop and organize their leadership structure, and set their own graduation requirements.

The Charter establishing each school is a contract describing the school's mission, goals, program, curriculum, methods of assessment, and means to measure success, which must be approved by the local school board.  However, unlike other public schools, charter schools must raise their own capital funds to pay for new facilities, equipment, and additional programs.  

The first charter school was created in Minnesota in 1992 after their state legislature passed the first charter law in 1991. Since that time, states across the nation have recognized the critical need to try new and innovative approaches to improving student achievement in our public schools while holding those schools accountable for how students learn. Charter schools deliver this combination of achievement and accountability and have the flexibility to try innovative ways of improving learning with the goal of sharing what works with the broader public school system so that all students benefit. 

The Center for Educational Reform reports that by 2009, there were more than 1,400,000 students enrolled in 4,600 charter schools nationwide.  In New Mexico, 83 charter schools have been founded since the Charter Schools Act (House Bill 888) authorized them in 1993 and currently those schools serve over 16,000 students. Monte del Sol is accredited by the New Mexico Public Education Department.
