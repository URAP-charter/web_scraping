Global Perspective

We encourage our students to explore the world. By engaging intellectually and experientially, they develop the understanding that New Mexicans are but one piece in a rich and complex global mosaic.

We anchor this global perspective by offering Spanish, French, and Japanese as second languages, as well as Spanish Language Arts and English Lanugage Development for those students who's first language is Spanish.  We personalize the experience with relationships and travel opportunities. Monte del Sol has established sister city schools in Oaxaca, Mexico, Tsuyama, Japan, Holguin, Cuba, and Nantes, France.

On our first overseas trip in 2004 a group of fifteen students and three teachers spent a week in Oaxaca living with local families, learning about their lives and culture. Since our founding, we have taken students to Japan, Spain, England and France five times and have welcomed students from some our sister schools.  Each summer, several of our students participate in the Bali Art Project in Indonesia along with students from other Santa Fe schools.  In 2009, five students spent a month in India on a trip organized by Sohini Das, our Fulbright Education exchange teacher from Kolkata.  In 2011, the Rose Simmons Memorial Fund was established to allow Monte del Sol students a chance to follow Rose's dream and attend the Youth Week at the pioneering ecological, educational community at Findhorn in Northern Scotland.  

Through a partnership with the United World College in Las Vegas, NM, our students had the opportunity to work with the next generation of international leaders from over 85 countries. Students and faculty from UWC worked with our students to build an inclusive school culture using the UWC's 'The Constructive Engagement of Conflict' curriculum.  In 2006, we were honored to host former President of Poland, Mr. Lech Walesa, as part of a conference put on by the United World College on our campus.    
 
A global perspective is woven through the history curriculum so that with real knowledge of the world around them, our students become more capable navigators of the currents of their lives and times. Future leaders must appreciate the diversity and interdependence of the global society. By broadening students' perspectives, we enrich their lives and ours.
