Board Members

The Ricardo Flores Magón Academy has selected Board Members from all backgrounds to help facilitate the school. Below are the current Board Members, meetings are held every third Monday of the month at 6pm. Please check the annual board calendar for the location.

Current Board Members

Gina Del Castillo
Board Chair 

Contract consultant and business owner Gina’s career spans over 30 years experience in nonprofit management including program development, building organizational capacity, fund development, strategic planning; board development and organization evaluation. Her passions include education and involvement in the Latino community. She has worked with nonprofit organizations in supporting their ability to sustain their place in community while building their foundation base. She has presented to organization on how important it is to re-evaluate their standing and organizational growth is necessary. She is a graduate of Regis University with a (BA) in Business and Sociology; attended Colorado Christian University Masters Program (MA) in Education specializing in Curriculum and Instruction. Gina is also consults one-to- one as a personal career and life coach. Currently, she has served on several boards and currently with Centro Humanitario and Ricardo Flores Magón Academy; and volunteers for other nonprofit organizations. She is a native of Denver, has one son, Andres; two of the most amazing grandchildren Marcos 21 and Isabelle 16 and is a co-caregiver of her 77 year old mother.

Jerry McElroy
Vice Board Chair 

Jerry McElroy is a principal in the Denver-based government relations firm, Ally Public Policy. Jerry McElroy worked 35 years for Colorado’s largest integrated health care system that included an 800-member physician group. Twenty-eight of those years were spent doing government relations work for the organization. His experience includes successful advocacy at the Colorado General Assembly, regulatory agencies and local governments on a variety of topics in the fields of business, health care and insurance with issues including insurance regulation, pharmacoeconomics, labor relations, health benefits, licensed professions, tort reform, public health, land use, economic development and senior issues. He has been closely involved with many different organizations with interests in health care and business issues such as the Colorado Association of Health Plans, the Denver Metro Chamber of Commerce, the Colorado Competitive Council, Business Council for Healthcare Competition, and the Colorado Association of Commerce and Industry. He has served on the boards of the Englewood Chamber of Commerce and the Denver Brass. Jerry is an expert on healthcare issues in the state and especially skilled in strategy and coalition building. He graduated from the University of Alabama in 1975 with a degree of Physician Assistant in Medicine and worked in that field for seven years before moving to the area of government relations.

Susan Cox
Secretary 

In Okinawa, it is believed that obtaining a balanced, happy life is reached through the ongoing development of four areas, like the legs of a chair. If any one of those “legs” is not reinforced daily, the chair falls over. One of those legs is intellectual development through ongoing education. The importance of education was consistently stressed in Susan’s home while growing up. The idea that education removed the barriers to dreams, unlocked doors of opportunity and provided a deeper understanding of the world in which we live was reinforced at every turn. With four school teachers in Susan’s family, she has been kept informed as to the challenges and successes of the education system in the state. And with over 25 years working at the state legislature representing various industries, she has been able to keep a close eye on changes to Colorado’s educational system, both K – 12 and higher education. From the very beginning, Susan loved going to school and learning new things every day. She continued her formal education to obtain a bachelor of arts degree in Speech Communication at Colorado State University (Go Rams!). And every day since, she reinforces that leg of her life chair.

Anna Halaburda

Anna entered Trenton State College in 1971, transferred to Livingston at Rutgers in 1972, took time off to hike the Appalachian Trail in 1973, got married and started a family and eventually returned to college at Monmouth University where she earned her BS in Accounting in 1980.

Anna has thirty five years of accounting experience in both the public and private sectors and has served as a Chief Financial Officer of an international company, manager of a regional CPA firm as well as being the president of the business consulting firm, Little Bear Consulting, Inc., in addition to operating her own accounting firms in Florida and Colorado.

In order to better serve her clients, Anna earned her Certified Financial Planner designation in 1987, obtained her Life and Health Insurance license in 2009 and completed the Certified Business Exit Consultant program in July of 2013, adding additional levels of skills and expertise.

TRiUMPH is distinguished by their approach to advising business owners as entrepreneurs with additional expertise in systems analysis, accounting, corporate formalities, tax and entity structuring. We find it necessary to understand the ultimate goal of each client as well as their drivers in order to properly advise them. We cannot help them get to where they want to go until we know where “there” is.

In addition we build exit planning strategies into our clients’ business and domestic plans so that future transitions such as the sale of a business interest, death or divorce are planned for so that adverse effects are minimized and benefits are maximized.

Ms. Halaburda has built exit strategies into her clients’ strategic plans for over two decades and now offers this service to the entire business community in conjunction with Pinnacle Equity Solutions Inc., (http://pinnacleequitysolutions.com/). With this professional association Anna continues to be on the forefront of both training and technology in serving her client’s exit planning, value building and M&A needs.

Anna has also served on various Boards of Directors of non-profits during her professional career and currently serves as a Board member of Swallow Hill Music Association in Denver, Colorado (http://swallowhillmusic.org/about/board-of-directors/) since 2004 and also serves as a Board member and Treasurer of Ricardo Flores Magon Academy (https://www.magonacademy.org/board-members/) since April 2014.

Anna is currently the managing partner of TRiUMPH Business Services, LLC, (http://www.triumphbusinessservices.com/) a business consulting, accounting and tax firm. With her partners Custo Avalos and Ramiro Avalos, TRiUMPH serves clients throughout the US, Central and South America.

 

Viviana Casillas
Board Member

Viviana Casillas is the Program Administrator for the Career Online High School program/service and for the Rodolfo “Corky” Gonzales Branch Library of the Denver Public Library. Viviana is in charge of managing the Career Online High School program, which allows individuals in the City and County of Denver to get their high school diploma. At the Rodolfo “Corky” Gonzales Branch Library she oversees programming, events, and library exhibits.
Born in Zacatecas Mexico, migrated to the United States at the age of nine, she calls Colorado her home. Viviana is a big believer in education and taking advantage of any opportunities that come across to reach her goal. Attended the Arapahoe Community College and obtained her Associates of Art with a focus on Management, later transferred to the University of Colorado in Denver, where she received her Bachelor of Science in Business Administration. In December 2016, she received Masters in Library and Information Science with a focus on Leadership and Management from Emporia State University via the Colorado Cohort. Viviana is no stranger to Customer and Public Service, having spent 9 years in customer service and over 13 years in public service, which taught her the importance of providing the best service and equal access to information to everyone.

Mattias O’Merea
Treasurer

Senior Vice President, Investment Banking | BB&T Capital Markets

Mr. O’Meara specializes in providing financing for charter schools throughout country. Mr. O’Meara has over a decade of experience working as an investment banker, and has completed transactions ranging from project financings, acquisitions, and derivative advisory for a variety of governmental and 501(c)(3) organizations.  Before joining BB&T, Mr. O’Meara was a Public Finance Banker at Wells Fargo.  Prior to joining Wells Fargo, Matt worked for a boutique investment bank that specialized in financing charter schools throughout the nation. In the course of his work, Matt has been responsible for the creation and oversight of several charter school research projects including a financial database that continues to be the industry standard for statistical information pertaining to charter school finance. He is frequent speaker at charter school conferences and the author of several articles pertaining to charter school financing. He has also served on charter school boards. He earned a B.A. in 2005 from the University of Notre Dame in South Bend, Indiana.

Jorge Castañada
Vice Chair  

Jorge Castañeda is the founder and owner of Castañeda Law. He is a licensed attorney in Arizona and Colorado and has been practicing law for 15-years. He received his Bachelor’s Degree in Business Administration from the University of San Diego and graduated from Arizona State University College of Law. Jorge began his legal career in 2001 at the Maricopa County Attorney’s Office. In 2006 Jorge went into private practice, where he began practicing criminal defense and began his immigration practice. Jorge is fluently bilingual and has extensive experience in both the criminal and immigration courts. He has made it his professional goal to provide honest and straightforward representation for his clients.   Jorge was born in Nogales, Sonora Mexico. His family immigrated when he was a child and he grew up in Nogales, Arizona. His personal background gives Jorge a unique perspective on the issues that affect the immigrant community.

email: jorge@lawyersazco.com
