Our Fourth Graders are Leading Healthy Lives With Some Help from Kerry Simmonds. ﻿

5/1/2016

0 Comments

 
Over the month of April, the fourth grade Bulldogs at Northwood Academy Charter School created many memorable moments.  After watching our mentor, Kerry Simmond’s  video on healthy living, we decided to set some healthy living goals for the month of April.  Each of our 88 students in the fourth grade came up with five goals revolving around healthy living.  The students’ goals dealt with the topics of healthy eating, hygiene, active play, education, spending time with their family, and being a better person.  The students tried to come up with measurable goals, so that they would be easy to assess at the end of the month. Also, the teachers made sure that the goals could realistically be accomplished. Every Friday, the students recorded their progress for the past week.  At the end of the four weeks, the students wrote a reflection piece that detailed whether or not they accomplished their goals. You can take a look at our Goal Organizer by clicking here.
​


Health Fair
The first activity that kicked off the month of healthy living was our school wide health fair. The fourth grade Bulldogs visited the health fair that was hosted by the seventh and eighth grade students at Northwood. The seventh and eighth grade students demonstrated to students the importance of exercising by lifting weights and dancing.  Outside vendors from local health care companies, hospitals, dental offices and colleges gave demonstrations and provided information about healthy living.  It was a very informative experience for all of the students in attendance.

Lankenau Hospital Visit
The next activity our fourth grade students participated in was a visit to Lankenau Hospital in Wynnewood, Pennsylvania.  All of the fourth grade Bulldogs visited Lankenau Hospital and received a lesson on healthy living.  Students were able to see a life-like skeleton named Pandora. They learned about different bones and how blood circulates throughout the human body. The fourth grade Bulldogs learned about the parts of the food plate.  They learned about what good foods they need to put in their bodies, and foods to stay away from.  At the end of the lesson, students had the chance to get out of their seats and participate in some aerobic exercises.  




#whitecard
The #whitecard activity for April 6th was a lot of fun for the students and teachers.  On April 5th, the teachers introduced the meaning behind #whitecard to their students.  Then, students brainstormed in small groups picture ideas for their small groups and the grade as a whole.  The students came up with some creative ideas that revolved around sports, friendship, and silliness.  Students posted their photos on Twitter and our school’s Instagram page. As a grade, all the students came together to form a huge 6 in the middle of our gymnasium.  All of the students proudly held up the white cards.  April 6th was a great day of fun and community for our fourth grade bulldogs.  Toward the end of the day, we noticed something on Twitter that became a highlight for us. Steve Mesler was called out for not posting his #whitecard picture before the Northwood Bulldogs.  Miss Cappuccino found the most humor in this.

Healthy Choices Game Show
The fourth grade Bulldogs at Northwood Academy Charter School had a blast creating and participating in the Healthy Choices Game Show Kahoot!  Miss Cappuccino and Mr. Burst’s brought their classes together to generate a Kahoot! that had ten questions with four answer choices for each question revolving around healthy living tips.  Miss Cappuccino’s class and Mrs. Gannon’s class invited two third grade classes to compete in the Healthy Choices Game Show Kahoot!, while Mr. Burst’s class and Mrs. Jacob’s classes invited two different third grade classes to compete in the Healthy Choices Game Show Kahoot!  The fourth grade Northwood Bulldogs’ goals for these lessons were to spread healthy living knowledge to the third grade, and have some friendly competition in the Kahoot! game.

Each of the third and fourth grade classes had at least five participants compete in the Healthy Choices Game Show Kahoot! The other students in the classroom were the audience members. The audience members held up signs and cheered for their classmates. The third and fourth grade Northwood Bulldogs had the pleasure of having Mr. Bonner as their host of the game. His comedic timing and energy made the experience even more memorable.  At the end of the event, the third graders were able to walk away with some great tips and information that will help them have a healthier lifestyle.  Also, they had an opportunity to see the fun and exciting activities that the fourth grade Northwood Bulldogs have been taken part in.

Healthy Living List
The fourth grade Bulldogs at Northwood Academy Charter School were eager to make a healthy living list of over 100 different healthy living choices.  On day 1 of this project, the four homerooms split up the work and each homeroom generated 25 choices that were entered on a Google Doc.  As the choices were entered, the
students and teachers discussed the benefits of the choices that were added to the list.  Also, students made sure that they were not repeating any healthy choices that the other classes came up with.

On day 2 of this project, the students in each classroom listed and drew pictures of their healthy living choices.  All of the students in the classes were able to participate in the creation of the poster.  When all four classes were finished with their portion of the poster, the posters were put together to form one large poster.

The students wanted to have some fun with the large poster.  The students took a group photo with all the participants involved.  Then, students were selected from each of the four classrooms to participate in an activity that would show the height of the poster. Students wanted to show the world how big the poster is in
comparison to fourth grade students’ heights.  The students laid down on the ground from head to toe.  The poster measured out to almost four Northwood Bulldogs.

The Northwood Bulldogs had a wonderful time planning and creating the poster of 100 different healthy living choices.  The students and classrooms worked well together as a team to meet the deadline of completion.  The teachers and students were all proud of their effort and perseverance.

You can take a look at our 100 Healthy Living Tips by clicking here.

Poster Contest
The International Day Of Sport For Development And Peace (IDSDP) poster contest was the last activity completed by the students this month.  Students were placed into groups of 3-4 students.  The students came up with a message about how sports made the world a better place.  Two students from Mr. Burst’s class measured out 24 inches of poster board for each group to complete their message.  The groups’ topics ranged from respect for others, fair play during sporting events, healthy living, and how sports make the world a better place for all genders and races.  The students worked well together generating a rough draft of their posters.  They made compromises and took the necessary steps to have their posters done on time.  After completing the posters, the students uploaded their pictures to Instagram and Twitter.  These were the hashtags that they used: #IDSDP2016 #iPlayforSDGS #sport4betterworld #powerofsport #NorthwoodChamps #dreambig


Last Words on April
In closing, the teachers of the fourth grade Bulldogs want the Classroom Champions community to know how much we admire and respect our students’ efforts.  This month, our students had state testing in ELA, Math, and Science.  Our students showed perseverance by completing all of the long and intense testing, they participated in many Classroom Champions’ events, and the students maintained good behavior as role models for the students of other grades in our school building.  Our students have made us so proud and we are excited for what the remaining days of the year have in store for us.

Teamwork Makes the Dream Work for our Northwood Cham﻿pions!

3/1/2016

0 Comments

 


February Post for Classroom Champions about Teamwork
This cold month of February started off with us listening to Kerry talk to us with one of her teammates.  They talked to us about how important it is to work together when you are on a team.  Kerry told us it is important not only support yourselves but to make sure that you are helping others along as well.  We talked about what makes a good teammate.  4th grade at Northwood said, “ A good teammate is communicating, having good sportsmanship, always having a positive attitude, encouraging one another, having respect for each other, and always trying your best.  

Throughout this month, us as teachers came up with different activities the students had to perform as a class.  They had to work together, to accomplish these different tasks.  It was so awesome to see two students who may have never talked to each other before really supporting and encouraging one another to persevere and accomplish a goal.  
            

We started out the first week with the simple task of getting in line in order of your Birthday’s without saying a word.  Some students were lost and others quickly jumped in and helped them to find a spot in line.  We then worked together as a whole group to pass the hula hoop around in the circle.  We played a balloon game, where we had to work together to keep the balloon off the floor but make sure in the process that every student touched it.  The students really enjoyed these activities as a whole class and they learned that the support of a teammate is very important.  We also have been working on our Social Studies Projects throughout the month, we are working with a partner to complete a Kahoot.  The lessons we have learned in our whole class activities we are able to carry to Social Studies when working with our partners.
 
We ended the month with a morning filled with different types of teamwork activities.  The students put themselves into groups of 5 and 6 students, they then traveled from classroom to classroom completing a variety of different activities.  They had to work together to stack a set of cups using just a rubber band and some string.  They had to get their teammates across the “minefield” blindfolded by just using directions.  This was an activity of trust as well as teamwork.  They had to work together with their teammates and others to get through the memory field.  They also completed puzzles, They solved a bank mystery and wrote short stories based on just a simple picture.  The students really enjoyed showing off their teamwork skills they have learned. Each student was presented with a certificate of completion at the end of Teamwork Day! We are super excited to share it with you Kerry and Classroom Champions.  

This month we utilized our space on our school website to share with parents and our school community what we were learning about this month. We also tweeted and share photos from our Twitter and Instagram accounts - @Northwoodchamps We were even featured on our school's Facebook page this month!

﻿ Northwood Academy Bulldogs and +Kerry Simmonds Show How Volunteering Can Impact Lives

12/22/2015

0 Comments

 
After setting short-term and long-term goals in September and learning the importance of being honest in October, Northwood Academy Bulldogs were eager to hear what Kerry Simmons had to say about the monthly topic of COMMUNITY in November.  Over the past month and a half, students in Mr. Burst's class, Mrs. Gannon's class, Mrs. Jacob's class and Miss Cappuccino's class worked hard to set a good example for the other students in the Northwood community.  Below, you can read about and see our adventures take place.  Along the way, you will see that the students were in for quite a surprise!
Lessons
We kicked off the month of November with a lesson on  the meaning of community.  Students began lesson one by discussing what it meant to be in a community.  Also, students discussed what constituted a strong community.  Students loved hearing how Kerry helped younger athletes improve their rowing through her guidance and training.   
        As a fourth grade community, we decided to keep a volunteering log of our service in the community.  Our next lesson went over the volunteering log and the teachers explained how to make an entry.  This would be our project that would be completed for Kerry’s monthly challenge.  The log included the date, time, and type of experience that students participated in.  Students brainstormed on how they could make a difference in their school community and surrounding neighborhood.  Another lesson on what constitutes volunteer work was taught to students.  The log followed the students’ volunteer work as a timeline, so Kerry could see the hard work that the students put in over the 7 weeks.


The first entry of our log started with our Day of Service on November 4th.  Our Day of Service started at 9 AM and lasted until 1 PM.  All 86 students of the fourth grade community participated in the day’s events.  Twenty students joined Mr. Burst on a trip to the Cradles to Crayons factory in Conshohocken, Pennsylvania.  Students were able to volunteer in the factory and do various activities that benefitted the less fortunate in the area.  The first activity students participated in was a card making activity for children that would be receiving new book bags from Cradles to Crayons.  Students drew a picture and wrote a warm message to the students.  Next, the students participated in the cleaning and matching of toys that would become gifts to children.  Students tested out the toys to make sure that they were in working order and had no excessive damage to them.  Lastly, students cleaned shoes for people of all ages.  Students were given a cleaning spray and sponge to clean the shoes.  The shoes were sized and categorized and then placed in a bin to be shipped out.  Students did all of the activities that they were assigned to do enthusiastically.  They were working along other volunteer groups from local companies.  Students were doing the same work as the adults.


Back at Northwood, the remaining students in the fourth grade participated in sewing activities for their  Day of Service.  Students designed and knit hats and pillows for the patients at the local Cancer Treatment Center.  Students were able to pick their fabric and make a design of their choosing for their project.  The sewing activities were completed over a series of additional classroom periods.


Over the course of the remaining weeks, students participated in many community activities around Northwood.  Every Tuesday, Mr. Burst’s class volunteered to help third grade students with their multiplication.  They taught strategies to the students and helped them drill.  Also, Mr. Burst’s class helped first grade students with their reading comprehension.


Mrs. Gannon's class worked closely with a first grade classroom in the building.  They helped the class with their holiday writing.  Also, Mrs. Gannon’s class helped first graders with a math lesson.  The lesson centered around making a Thanksgiving menu.  The classes had a lot of fun working together!       
Mrs. Jacob's class wrote letters to soldiers and thanked them for their service.  The letters wanted to carry on the meaning of Veteran’s Day.  Also, Mrs. Jacob's class made holiday cards for cancer patients.  They wanted to send a warm message and holiday cheer to their fellow Philadelphians.    To finish out their service, Mrs. Jacob’s class read to kindergarten students.  The younger students found joy in the older students reading to them.


Miss Cappuccino's class worked with a class of third grades to collect the recycling bins from all the classrooms in the building.  Also, Miss Cappuccino’s class helped first graders with a math lesson.  They helped a different first grade class with their Thanksgiving menus.     The classes had fun finding out the total bill for the feast.
    The wrap up lesson for Community asked students to finish their volunteer logs and reflect on their hours of service that was completed.  Students were asked to reflect on the following questions:
  • What is the most important thing that you have taken away from this community service project that you would want to share with Kerry Simmons?
  • What was your most memorable moment during the community service project?
  • What would you change about our community service project?
  • How can we build upon what we’ve already started in the months of November and December?

OMG!  The Northwood Bulldogs Meet Kerry!
    During the month of November, all of the fourth grade students at Northwood were able to video chat with Kerry!  Teachers helped students brainstorm questions that they could ask Kerry about her goals, the honesty lesson, community, rowing, growing up, the Olympics, etc.  Students were greatly anticipating this day.  In Mr. Burst’s class, students were quite nervous to meet their mentor.  They looked on in awe as Kerry joined the video chat and greeted the students.  The conversations were insightful and students took a lot away from the experience.  Students loved hearing about Kerry’s adventures and her childhood.

October was all about Honesty! ﻿

11/2/2015

0 Comments

 

October= Honesty!  Fourth grade at Northwood kicked off the month of October on the 2nd by discussing what it meant to be honest.  Fourth graders believe that being honest it not telling a lie.  We talked about times when maybe in our lives we have a told a lie.  We even talked about how sometimes you may tell a lie but it’s for a joke, you just have to be careful because sometimes people’s feeling can be hurt even though that was not the intention of the prank or joke.  After we discussed what it meant to be honest we got to hear from Kerry.  We were so excited to get to listen to her coming to us all the way from France!  She helped us understanding how honesty can help us in our daily life and that the most important person to be honest with is yourself!  We talked about being honest when it comes to our goals, if not we are making it harder for ourselves to achieve those set goals.  
     After that we decided that it would be fun to make skits or plays, (we just finished reading a play in reading class) to show Kerry that we understand what Honesty means, and why we should be honest.  We talked and considered times when kids may be dishonest in their lives and some of the consequences for those actions.  We talked about things that may occur in school and times at home.  We talked about how consequences could be different for people at home and at school.  At home it is your parents or guardians who are in charge of your consequence were as at school it is the teacher or principal that guides that consequence.  
      The students have been working very hard to get their plays written and have practiced very hard to be ready to perform them.  Some are very nervous to be on camera and even more nervous to have Kerry watching them.  We are constantly asking ourselves if we are being honest!  Are we being honest with ourselves, or classmates, teacher and parents.
