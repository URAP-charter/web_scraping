Teacher Angie Maxson

Welcome to our Planet Protectors Second Grade

Angie has been teaching at NCSA since the year 2000 when she moved from Santa Cruz County to Nevada County in order to teach at Nevada City School of the Arts.  This is the place where she feels surrounded by dedicated professionals who love to enrich the lives of students. Angie has a deep love for teaching about the environment, which she fostered while teaching outdoor science at several schools up and down the Northern California coast before she decided to move her classroom indoors.  She graduated from Humboldt State University in 1996 with her California Teaching Credential.

It is important to her to share her love of learning with her students.  She is often sharing real life experiences with them in order to help them make a new connection or understanding.  She likes to try to tap into the world of her students’ lives to better understand where they are coming from so that she may support their learning experiences in the best possible way.

Angie loves her job and is thankful to get to spend her days with beautiful open minds that are so ready to learn.  “Who could ask for more than the eagerness of a second grader?”

 

Two Important Rules Angie believes we should say each day;  I am a miracle! I am ten times smarter than I think I am!

Below you will find information about what we do throughout the year.

There are 20 students in our class.  We have one aide who helps throughout our day.  We hold literacy reading class in small groups and math in both small and whole group depending upon the activity.  We do many science lessons and often enjoy them with our second grade partner class.  We are a class of authors writing daily.

A highlight of our classroom experience throughout the year is the myriad of enrichment activities.  The students attend two visual arts classes, one handwork class, one music class and two movement classes a week!  These classes are taught by amazing specialists well versed in their art.
