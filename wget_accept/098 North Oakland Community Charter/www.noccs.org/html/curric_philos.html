Educational Philosophy

“Respect for children and their learning” is our first core principle and it guides everything we do.


NOCCS was founded on this simple but powerful idea—that our school’s mission and design would support teaching and learning that deeply respects children’s minds, their natural curiosity, multiple intelligences, and their sense of wonder about the world.   


Ours is a learning environment where children can develop their imagination and lend their hearts and minds to pursuing important ideas and questions about the world around them.


Critical to having respect for children and their learning is the notion that we hold high expectations for all students.  We believe that all children are capable of learning and demonstrating understanding and skills related to high and rigorous academic standards.  High expectations for students’ academic, social, and emotional growth and achievement, rooted in and tied to standards, is evident in rigorous demonstrations of student work.  Students are regularly challenged to revise their work focusing on improvement through a variety of lenses from structure and craft to content; make their thinking visible to others through a variety of modalities from writing and problem solving to the creative expression through the arts; explore a diversity of perspectives on a given topic from the dominant culture to the voices of those who are under-represented in our histories; use processes of inquiry to explore, reveal, and solve problems; and to demonstrate their learning and understanding in a real-life context.


We also know that children, educators, and families do their best work in a caring community of learners that cultivates in students a deep respect for each other’s work, experience, contributions, and perspectives, and where children and teachers alike nurture supportive relationships.


We believe that communities are shaped through shared commitment, and that powerful teaching and learning helps learners develop shared goals and outcomes that can best be accomplished by drawing on the talents, interests, and efforts of all students, their families, and the educators who work with them.  Through sharing and critiquing teacher work, teachers improve their craft in a collegial and respectful forum.  Teaching students to critique their own and each other’s work enables them to internalize high standards and produce higher quality work.


Teaching for Understanding, our school-wide approach to teaching and learning, allows us to put these core beliefs into practice.


NOCCS uses Teaching for Understanding, a teaching framework developed by teachers and researchers at the Harvard Graduate School of Education, including leading educators and cognitive psychologists such as Howard Gardner and David Perkins.  In Teaching for Understanding classrooms, teachers carefully design thought-provoking tasks that call on students to demonstrate a deep understanding of important subject matter.  Using various forms of inquiry that engage their curiosity and interests, students ask probing questions, test their theories, make inferences, connect what they learn to the real world, and ultimately, apply their understanding in unique, real-world, public contexts. 


While not a prescribed curriculum, Teaching for Understanding guides teachers in developing academically rigorous learning units that require students to engage in higher order thinking about important content matter in various disciplines.  Teaching for Understanding is not curricula or standards, rather it supports our teachers in organizing California’s content standards into coherent lessons and units of study. 


The result is adventurous, understanding-rich, project-based educational model that engages children in a deep exploration that builds on their knowledge and curiosity, leading to an inquiry into the big ideas and questions from science, language arts, mathematics, and the humanities.  The aim is to nurture the habits and heart and mind that children need to be life-long learners.
