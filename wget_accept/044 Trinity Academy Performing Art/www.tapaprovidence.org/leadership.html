LEADERSHIP

Administrative Team 
Nanci DeRobbio, Head of School
Mrs.DeRobbio@TAPAprovidence.org
With 30 years of experience in the field of Education, Mrs. Nanci DeRobbio is a leader of leaders in urban education with extensive experience in instructional leadership with an emphasis on interdisciplinary studies and arts integration in education.  Thirteen years of her experience has been in administration of schools, all with a passion and focus on her work as an advocate for education and the power of the arts. Life has led Mrs. DeRobbio down a path of experiences with people from all walks of life, from around the world, and from the streets of the cities, and schools that she has worked in. (In addition to raising four children as a single mother in Providence, Rhode Island). She brings passion to the community, with her affiliation with organizations such as the NAACP, the Urban League of Rhode Island, Resources for Rhode Island Education, League of RI Charter Schools, and through service on the multiple Boards for Arts Organizations to include: Opera Providence, Tsetse Initiative, and the Natasha Love Foundation; with outreach opportunities for socio-economically disadvantaged youth and new and emerging talent. As with all non-profit organizations, much of her energy goes into raising funds to keeping the arts and education experience accessible, and using the arts in education as a catalyst for social change, for equality, and for equity in our vibrant city of Providence. Mrs. DeRobbio received her M.A. in Educational Leadership from the University of Rhode Island, her Teaching Certification at the University of California, Riverside, and her B.A. in Liberal Studies from California State University, Dominguez Hills. 
Elizabeth Richards-Hegnauer, Assistant Head of School & Dean of School Culture
﻿﻿Ms.Richards-Hegnauer@TAPAprovidence.org﻿﻿
Liz is a founding Administrator at TAPA, and was part of the team who wrote TAPA's Education Plan and saw the school through its initial approval process. At TAPA, Liz oversees all aspects of student life, including building TAPA's academically rigorous artistic culture and leading all discipline. She also leads TAPA's branch of RI's Shakespeare in the City program, and is the Adviser to "R-Cubed," a Class of 2016 Advisory. Prior to arriving at TAPA, Liz completed Bachelor's Degrees in English and Education from Vassar College, and a Master's Degree in Urban Education Policy from Brown University. Liz also taught English Language Arts at the middle and high school levels, and worked at the Rhode Island Department of Education. Liz has extensive experience in the performing arts, having served as the manager of a 180-seat black box theater and as a co-founder of a nonprofit community theatre group. Outside of TAPA, Liz is one of the leaders of the Teens in Action, the Grace Church youth group, the mom of a teenager, and a viciously competitive player of team trivia. 
Andrew MacMannis, Director of Learning Supports
Mr.Macmannis@TAPAprovidence.org
Andy MacMannis oversees all support services at TAPA. This ranges from Special Education, to TAPA's Title One programming, to all after-school programming. Additionally, Andy leads data-based decision-making at the school, is the test coordinator for all state-wide assessments, and is one of the school's special educators. Prior to TAPA, Andy attended Providence College, majoring in Elementary/Special Education. While at PC, he worked for the YouthRAP after-school program in Providence. From there, he attended Brown University, where he received a Master's degree in Urban Education Policy. Andy has also recently completed coursework at Rhode Island College and Bridgewater State College in order to obtain his certification as a Special Education Administrator. Andy joined TAPA during it's first year as a math/science/special education instructor, and has been the advisor to "the Mac and Cheese" and the coach for "Star Mole Track" since year one. Outside of school, Andy is a singer-songwriter, who plays guitar and bass. He is also an avid comic book reader and loves to travel!

Erin Sullivan, Curriculum and Instruction Coordinator
Ms.Sullivan@TAPAprovidence.org
Originally from Fort Lauderdale, Florida Erin Sullivan came to Rhode Island to attend Providence College. In 2003 she graduated with dual Bachelor of Arts degrees in Elementary and Special Education. By December of 2004 Erin graduated again, from Providence College, with a Master's Degree in Education with a concentration in Literacy. Since graduation Erin has worked in many school districts including Providence, Holyoke, MA and Middleboro, MA as both a general education teacher as well as a special education teacher. As a member of these districts Erin has served as Department Head for the English Language Arts Department, a member of the assistant superintendent's team on curriculum development in cooperation with the federal government and provided feedback and assistance in the development of the districtwide assessments. Erin was introduced to the TAPA team as the ELA curriculum consultant and expanded the position to work with teachers within the school as an instructional coach and curriculum coordinator. She shares her love of learning and exploration with her family. 
Patricia Hawkridge, Dean of the Arts
Ms.Hawkridge@TAPAprovidence.org
Patricia Hawkridge holds an MFA in Dramatic Arts with a concentration in Acting & Speech from The George Washington University. She is the founder/artistic director of LaVoce: Theatre That Speaks and the former Dean of the Arts at Beacon Charter High School for the Arts in Woonsocket. Prior to joining the administrative staff at TAPA, Pat was an Associate Professor of Theatre and Chairman of the Department of Theatre Arts at Salve Regina University. Pat has enjoyed directing over 35 productions; and as an actress, Pat has performed with the Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts, Horizons Theatre, Silver Spring Stage and Round House Theatre all in the Washington, DC area and Barker Playhouse, Gamm Theatre, NewGate Theatre, LaVoce and OUTLOUD Theatre all in Rhode Island. As an educator, Pat has taught theatre and speech classes for Salve Regina University, Providence College, Brown University, University of Rhode Island, Community College of Rhode Island, Rhode Island College, Wheaton College and the George Washington University. Pat also serves as a vocal coach the for RI Department of Education, as well as area colleges, high schools, local theatres and businesses in RI. In June, 2013, Pat completed her Certificate of Advanced Study in Arts Integration from the Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts. 
Eliza Bryant, College Guidance Counselor
Ms.Bryant@TAPAProvidence.org
Eliza is a licensed school counselor who recently returned to live and work in her home state of Rhode Island, where she has supported students in Providence, Newport, and Central Falls.  Previously, Eliza ran an academic support and college preparation site within the Boston Public Schools.  She also serves as the summer guidance counselor for URI's First Star Program, where she helps youth in foster care complete all aspects of the college application process.  She is excited to work with every TAPA student & family in pursuit of their college and career dreams.  Outside of TAPA, Eliza loves running, rowing, pickup basketball, music, and working at the same South County farm that hired her in high school.  She speaks a comfortable level of Spanish and loves any opportunity to learn more!
Melissa DaSilva, LICSW, School Social Worker
Ms.DaSilva@TAPAprovidence.org
Melissa DaSilva is a graduate of Rhode Island College, where she also completed her Master's Degree in Social Work with a specific focus on School Social Work. During her Bachelor's program at RIC, she received the Anthony E. Ricci Award. The award recognized the work she had done in her community with children, where she re-established an after school program and developed a summer program. Ms. Melissa and her dog Brutus are certified in Pet Assisted Therapy. She and Brutus are also advocates for preventing the abuse toward animals. Ms. Melissa enjoys singing, reading, volunteering at the local animal shelter and creating art out of items others see as trash. Above all she enjoys listening and helping others.
