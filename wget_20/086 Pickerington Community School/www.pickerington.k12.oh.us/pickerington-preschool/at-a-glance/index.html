About Pickerington Preschool

Locations

Overview

The Pickerington Preschool is a program that enhances and encourages learning for children with disabilities and typically developing children (peer models). We welcome children ages 3-5 years of age, while offering a range of services free to children with a qualifying disability. Typically developing children (peer models) are enrolled on a tuition basis. All children are provided with a typical preschool experience and the opportunity to interact with children of all abilities. See information on “Identifying Peer Models and Special Needs Students” below.

The program is operated according to the Ohio Department of Education standards, federal mandates for the Education of Individuals with Disabilities in Education Act, and state licensure rules and regulations. Preschool teachers must hold a license for the Early Education of the Handicapped issued by the Ohio Department of Education.

The philosophy of the program emphasizes developmentally-appropriate activities.  The preschool has adopted the Creative Curriculum. Within this context, children with  disabilities receive play-based, individualized, developmentally-appropriate  interventions, including child- and teacher-directed activities. Typically-developing  children are considered to be an integral component of the program as role models for instructional and social interactions.

All program services are operated according to the Ohio Department of Education Rules for the Education of preschool children with disabilities and state licensure rule.

Program Options

There are several options for parents wanting to enroll their children in Pickerington Preschool, including center-based classrooms, itinerant service, and providers.

Center-based classrooms

  • Maximum of eight children with special needs and up to eight typically developing children per classroom
  • Special education teacher and assistant(s)
  • Related services may include speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, orientation and mobility, audiology and transportation.

Itinerant

Itinerant options provide service delivery in the home or at other preschool sites, such as Head Start. This service is available for one hour per week.

Providers

  • Special education teacher
  • Speech-language pathologist
  • Occupational therapist
  • Physical therapist

Dropping off and Picking Up Students

DROP OFF:

Staff will be available 5 minutes prior to start time and remain at the doors until 5 minutes past start time.

If you arrive beyond that time, please take your child to the front office.  The building secretary will notify the classroom and send an assistant to escort your child to the classroom.

** Tussing Parents- you must park your car in the front parking lot and bring your child to the MARKED SIDE DOORS. 

PICK UP:

Staff will be available beginning at 10:40 or 2:40 at the side doors for dismissal.  Please do not go to the main office unless you are late.

If you arrive beyond that time, please go to the front office.  The building secretary will notify the classroom and an assistant will bring your child to you.

**Tussing Parents: You must park your care in the front lot and pick up your child at the MARKED SIDE DOORS.

 

Info about Early Learning Development Standards

On October 9, 2012, the State Board of Education adopted Ohio’s Entry Early Learning and Development Standards in all domains of school readiness to reflect the comprehensive development of children beginning at birth to kindergarten entry.  The development of these standards represented a collaboration between state agencies serving young children.  The state agencies worked with national experts and writing teams made up of Ohio-based content experts and stakeholders to revise and expand the standards.

To learn more, please visit the ODE website.ODE Early Learning Standards
