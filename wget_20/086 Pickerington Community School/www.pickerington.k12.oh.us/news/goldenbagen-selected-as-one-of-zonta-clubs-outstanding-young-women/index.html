Goldenbagen Selected as one of Zonta Club’s Outstanding Young Women

Pickerington High School Central senior Emerson Goldenbagen has been selected as one of 12 Zonta International Club of Columbus Outstanding Young Women in Central Ohio for 2017. According to Zonta Club co-chairs, she has been chosen on the basis of her scholastic achievements, leadership and commitment to service in the community and school.

Zonta Club International is a leading global organization of professionals who dedicate their time to empower women around the world through service and advocacy. One of its service initiatives is called Outstanding Young Women. This initiative is for Central Ohio high school senior women nominated by guidance counselors to gain recognition for all their dedication and hard work.

Goldenbagen has always been active in and out of school but made service her priority once she got into high school. She even stopped participating in volleyball to dedicate that time to what she saw as an opportunity to help others.

She has taken particular interest iRun for Jonah and has become known for her participation with the event. iRun is a 5k run hosted annually to help raise awareness and money to help with medical expenses for a child in the community. For the past two years she has been a student committee chairman and helped plan the event.

In addition to iRun, Goldenbagen also has been president of National Honor Society, vice president of student council, and active in the Sunny Side-Up organization.

“Whenever I get recognized for the community service and the work I do around the school, it is never just me that is being recognized,” Goldenbagen said. “It sometimes can be hard to take, just because I get to work with so many incredible peers that are just as creative and willing to help and want to make a change in the community. It is very humbling, and I am glad that I can be recognized as well as all the other peers and teachers I have been able to work with.”

Goldenbagen plans to attend The Ohio State University and earn a degree in secondary math education. She plans to continue service while she is in college and resume putting others before herself.

“I just want to help make an impact in some way,” she said. “It doesn’t matter if it would be big or small, but if it just helps someone or something, or a group of people in some way, that is my dream, to help others. “

PHSC school counselor Jennifer Dashner nominated Goldenbagen for the Zonta International recognition.

“I’m lucky to have Emerson on my student caseload,” Dashner said. “She is a wonderful student who strives for academic excellence and shows maturity, leadership and extraordinary personal character on a consistent basis.  She was nominated by more than one staff member for the Zonta Club of Columbus scholarship. Zonta International looks for leaders in service and young women who give back.  Emerson fits this description.”

Zonta will host the honoring reception on March 12 at Worthington Hills Country Club. Not only will the 12 recipients be recognized, but scholarship awards will be presented to offset college costs to three of the 12 Outstanding Young Women.

Best of luck to Emerson. You make Pickerington Schools proud!
