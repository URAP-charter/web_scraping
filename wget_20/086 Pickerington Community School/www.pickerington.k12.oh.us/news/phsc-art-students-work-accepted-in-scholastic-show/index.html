Jan 27, 2017

PHSC Art Students Work Accepted in Scholastic Show

Congratulations to the art students from Pickerington High School Central for being accepted into the 2017 Scholastic Art Show.

The Central Ohio region received 1,381 individual submissions and 66 portfolio submissions from students in grades 7 through 12. This year, five pieces will be recommended for the American Visions award. Regional acknowledgements will go to 63 pieces at the Gold Key level, 108 pieces at the Silver Key level, and 262 will receive Honorable Mention certificates.

Congrats to these talented young people!

Gold Key

  • Karisah Brown, SoLuna Digital Art: Gold Key
  • Josh Chaney, Fishin’ For Some Fission Photography
  • Montana Widener, Chainmail Sleeve with Leather Jewelry

Silver Key

  • Tavia Eagle, Weight of The World Drawing and Illustration
  • Erin Donahue, Life, Animated Art Portfolio
  • Samantha Lassahn, Transformation Film & Animation
  • Dalton Souder, Trecheng Breth Féne Jewelry

Honorable Mention

  • Sienna Float, Reincarnation Mixed Media
  • Josh Chaney, Street Santa Photography
  • Sienna Float, Reincarnation Mixed Media
  • Damarius Ingram, Doors Ceramics & Glass
  • Devin Luby, Coiled Ceramics & Glass
  • Shelby Miller, Rooted In The Cross Painting
  • Shelby Miller, Jesus Gang Sign Digital Art

The exhibit will run weekdays from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. from Feb. 13 through 23 in Columbus College of Art & Design Acock Gallery, Canzani Center.

All students who receive a Gold Key, Silver Key, or Honorable Mention will be listed in the exhibition program book, which will be available in the gallery.

The Scholastic Art & Writing Awards are the nation’s longest-running and most prestigious recognition program for creative teens in grades 7–12.

Last year, students submitted nearly 320,000 works of visual art and writing to the Scholastic Awards. More than 85,000 works were recognized at the regional level and celebrated in local exhibitions and ceremonies. The top art and writing at the regional level were moved onto the national stage, where 2,500 works earned National Medals. National Medalists and their educators were celebrated at the National Ceremony at Carnegie Hall in New York City.
