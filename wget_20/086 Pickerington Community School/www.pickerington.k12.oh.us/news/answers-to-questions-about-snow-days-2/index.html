Answers to Questions about Snow Days

When the weather gets bad or when temperatures drop below zero, we get questions about our closing and delay policies. There are times the decision to close or delay is obvious, but it often is a judgment call based on numerous factors. It is never a decision we take lightly.

Here are answers to some of the frequently asked questions.

When is the decision made?

We strive to make the decision to delay or close by 5:30 a.m. or earlier so we can notify the community. However, it is not always possible to make a decision by that time. Developing weather conditions and issues with school buses sometimes mean those messages are delivered after 5:30 a.m. For that reason, we encourage parents to stay tuned to district social media, local media and keep your phone close at hand for updates.

What goes into our decision-making process?

First and foremost, our priority is the safety of our children. Everyone agrees that, assuming the roads are safe, having children in the classroom is preferable to canceling classes for the day.

On snowy days, we begin evaluating road conditions before 4 a.m. Pickerington is a large and geographically diverse district, with some significant hills and winding roads. Some areas are cleared of snow before others; just because the roads are clear in one part of the district does not mean they are clear in others. We also take the projected forecast for additional snow into consideration.

At other times, temperature is the issue. We take multiple factors into consideration here, too. There is no set temperature that determines a school closure. We look at current and projected temperatures and wind chill during bus pick-up time, and make our decision based on the factors at hand. We take into consideration the safety of those students who wait for the bus or walk to school. However, cold temperatures also can make it difficult to start buses, especially those running on diesel fuel. The buses are one reason the decision to delay school on cold days might not be made until the morning.

Communicating with parents

When the decision to cancel or delay school is made, the district works as quickly as possible to communicate that message to the community.

Our social media feeds (Facebook and Twitter) and our website are often the first places you can hear about closures. We contact the local TV stations as early as possible.

We also use Infinite Campus as our phone and email notification system. Parents must be sure their communication preferences are up to date to receive these calls and emails. Got to http://www.pickerington.k12.oh.us/parent-portal/ and click on “Updating your Infinite Campus Info and Contact Preferences” for full details. You MUST do this through your parent portal, not your child’s student portal.

Why didn’t I get a call/email?

There are a few reasons you might not have received a particular message sent by the district. The following at the two main reasons:

Who is the message intended for? For a school delay that only affects certain grades or certain schools, we would typically only send that phone message to parents of affected students.

Parent preferences. Parents must set up their communication preferences in their parent Infinite Campus portal.  This is where parents can indicate how they want to receive emails, texts and telephone messages from the district and from their child’s school.

How will we make up snow days?

The following plan has been approved by the Board of Education for the 2016-17 school year.

  • If the district takes any calamity days before Feb. 20, 2017, (Presidents Day) we will use that day as a make-up day, regardless of whether the district has reached the five-day limit of excusable days.
  • Days 2-4 will be identified as “blizzard bag” days. Teachers will provide these work-at-home lessons for students to complete when not in class.
  • If the district takes a fifth calamity day before April 17, 2017, we will use that day as a make-up day.
  • Days 6-10: Students are excused from attendance.

Detailed info on Blizzard Bags and school closings can be found on our website, http://www.pickerington.k12.oh.us/snow-calamity-days/
