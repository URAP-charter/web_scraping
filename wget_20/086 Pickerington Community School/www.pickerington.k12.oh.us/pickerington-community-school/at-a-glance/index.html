About Pickerington Community School

Pickerington Community School was launched four years ago with an important mission: Keep high school students who at are risk of dropping out of school in the classroom, and prepare them for college and career.

As of October 2015, the school has graduated more than 200 students and continues to open the doors of opportunity through a highly individualized approach to education.

PCS is sponsored by Pickerington Schools and is housed at Pickerington High School North. The program is open to students from Pickerington Schools, as well as all contiguous school districts. Thus, courses also are open to students in Reynoldsburg, Columbus, Lancaster, Bloom Carroll, Groveport, Canal Winchester and Liberty Union schools.

The Community School’s primary mission is to meet the needs of students who are at risk of not graduating, are credit deficient, or are struggling with traditional public schools. It addresses the needs of students who have attendance problems or other issues that may cause them to be at risk of not graduating.

But the program also creates an alternative for students seeking an education that works around a work schedule or family issues. It’s even for students who are trying to graduate early.

How can PCS serve so many different kinds of students? Because each student’s curriculum is customized, based on his or her specific needs.

That individual attention begins on day one, as the PCS staff works with each student to create a personalized career graduation plan. At that point, most students are assigned to one or two courses. Student-athletes are required to take at least five courses leading to graduation in order to be eligible to play sports.

After a plan is developed, each student works at his or her own pace. Students have access to the online curriculum 24 hours a day. Students who are struggling with a course can take the time needed to ensure they have mastered the information before moving on to another class. Students seeking an accelerated program that will help them graduate early might push through a single course in a few weeks.

PCS has a highly qualified teaching staff who are certified in the content areas they teach. Those teachers work one-on-one with students, checking to ensure they are meeting their weekly goals.

Sponsoring a community school has created numerous benefits for Pickerington school district. Offering a dropout prevention and credit recovery program helps us ensure every student has continued support from the schools.

Giving students this kind of individualized educational alternative also has increased the graduation rates for our high schools. Graduation rates are among the key criteria the district is evaluated on by the state.

Our program is designed to help all kids succeed. It is an alternative for students who have not previously experienced success in a traditional classroom setting, and the customized programs are ideal for students who need flexibility in their education.

For the district, PCS is one more way we show that every student matters.

For many students, PCS is a much-needed light at the end of the tunnel.

Know a high school student who might benefit from an individualized education developed by the PCS team of educators? Information about the school and enrollment can be found under Enrollment.

Our Mission

The mission of the Pickerington Community School (PCS) is to provide students with a customized and personalized educational experience leading to high school graduation and meaningful post high school employment, or successful transition to post high school education. The community school will provide educational opportunities for learners in the areas of academic development, civic leadership, and a lifetime of creative work.

The mission will be accomplished by:

1. Developing partnerships between PCS, area businesses, contractors, government agencies, cultural and civic organizations, and local post secondary institutions.

2. Engaging students in a curriculum that is specifically designed for students in grades 9-12 and at risk of not completing high school.

3. Using resources available within the economic corridors of the Pickerington/Reynoldsburg/ Columbus communities.

4. Having each student complete an individual career and graduation plan.

The PCS Difference

The Pickerington Community School is a dropout prevention/credit recovery high school that seeks to provide an academic environment that focuses on the individual strengths and interests of each student. Teaching and learning is personalized and customized to meet each student’s needs. The Pickerington Community School is dedicated to providing a learning environment that supports each student by developing a student’s academic competencies in all core academic areas.
