Ridgeview Gearing up to Make A Difference

 Ridgeview Junior High School believes that learning happens both inside and outside of the classroom. We believe in providing students with authentic learning opportunities that provide real world connections. By participating in activities in the Columbus and Pickerington communities, we align with our STEM focus of service learning. By learning through service, the students will make connections through multiple disciplines and engage in innovative thinking and problem solving. We believe that service learning also promotes and builds positive relationships between our school and the community.

On May 22nd, Ridgeview students and staff will go out into the community and serve in a variety of ways by collecting food for the Food Pantry, cleaning up debris in the parks, painting and sprucing up area businesses, washing police and fire vehicles, packaging meals for Haiti, sorting clothing donations, and much more.  We are excited to be able to provide our students with this opportunity for service and growth.

All students and adult volunteers will have the opportunity to purchase a T-shirt to wear on Service Learning Day.   They are only $5 and will help make our work crews highly visible within the community.  If you would like to purchase one or more shirts, please download the T-SHIRT ORDER FORM or pick one up on the office.  You also have the opportunity to make a donation of a shirt for a student who may not be able to purchase one due to financial constraints, as well as making a donation, if you choose to do so, toward the expense of a meal packaging for Haiti.

Start With HELLO Week

Social isolation is the overwhelming feeling of being left out, lonely, or treated like you are invisible. As a result, many further pull away from society, struggle with learning and social development and/or choose to hurt themselves or others. Start With Hello teaches students,
grades 2-12, the skills they need to reach out to and include those who may be dealing with chronic social isolation and create a culture of inclusion and connectedness within their school or youth organization.
Start With Hello Week at Ridgeview
During the week of February 6-10, 2017, hundreds of schools and youth organizations from across the United States will participate in Start With Hello Week.

Start With Hello Week brings attention to the growing epidemic of social isolation in our schools and communities, and empowers young people to create a culture of inclusion and connectedness within their school or youth organization.During this week our School Counselors and Social Work Interns have planned a week of activities to go along with this initiative culminating with “No one Eats Alone Day” on Friday the 10th.

NUMATS

Northwestern University’s Midwest Academic Talent Search (NUMATS) is a research-validated program that utilizes above-grade-level assessment, as a means of gifted testing, to help parents and educators better understand their students’ educational needs. NUMATS allows eligible students to take internationally recognized tests before the grade levels at which they are normally administered. The PSAT™8/9 test, designed for students in grades 8 and 9, is administered to students in grades 3 through 6. SAT® and ACT®, designed for students in grades 11 and 12, are administered to students in grades 6 through 9. NUMATS identifies academic ability, measures growth and connects students to resources and opportunities for advanced students.

For more information go to http://www.ctd.northwestern.edu/program/numats

Upcoming Opioid Design Challenge

Ohio is facing a major opioid overdose crisis. And as many Ohio families fight opioid abuse, the impact cascades into the learning environments in Ohio schools. This year, several Ohio science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) schools will complete design challenges aimed at understanding and eliminating Ohio’s opioid crisis. A design challenge is a multi-week project in which students learn by creating new solutions to real-world problems. Design challenges offer an approach to problem-based learning that can go deep into content areas while offering students a wide range of questions to consider. Ridgeview will be kicking off this design challenge in the next couple weeks. Students will work in teams to come up with their solutions to the national problem. They will first work within their teams and then compete with other teams to determine design challenge winners in the spring.

Message from the Principal

Dear Ridgeview families,

Welcome Back and Happy New Year! I hope everyone had a wonderful break! As we start the New Year, I wanted to take this time to remind all Ridgeview families of a couple of things during the winter season. As the weather turns for the worst, we can expect school delays and/or closures. As always, the district will contact TV and radio stations to inform them of any cancellations or delays. The district will also use social media and Infinite Campus Messenger to get this information out. Please make sure that your contact information is up to date on Infinite Campus so you can receive these and all important messages. For more information about delays, closures and/or blizzard bags, please visit Pickerington Local School District’s webpage at http://www.pickerington.k12.oh.us/snow-calamity-days/.

Ridgeview Junior High School is open at 6:45 am for students who get dropped off early. Students need to enter through the cafeteria doors by the Welcome Center. Students who arrive early will sit in the cafeteria until dismissed to classes at 7am. If you want to beat the traffic jam, I suggest dropping your student off before 7am. This truly makes a difference in keeping our staff and students safe. Please do not cut through the parking lot. There are a lot of staff members and students walking across the parking lot in the morning. With the weather getting worse and no daylight till later in the morning, I want to make sure everyone is safe. Again thank you to those who continue to help in this process. J

The new hallway traffic flow pattern has been working better than expected. We know some students do not like walking a little farther to class; however, the flow of the hallways has helped with the congestion. We all feel that the hallways are safer because of this. Please encourage your student to follow the hallway patterns. In addition, please remind them to get to their class as quickly as they can. We have allowed those who have to travel farther a couple of extra minutes to get to class. No student should be more than couple of minutes late to class. This includes lunch.

Interims will be posted by 4pm on Friday afternoon, January 5th. If you have any questions or concerns, please contact the teacher. The second trimester ends on February 17th, so there are six more weeks for grades to be improved or maintained.

Finally, I wanted to take this time to thank you for all of your support and for sharing your student with us this school year. I hope everyone has a great 2017!

Eric Koch, Principal

Update Petermann Transportation app

Before the end of this year, parents should replace the current “bus tracker” mobile application with Petermann Transportation’s new and improved version.

Petermann Transportation has updated its mobile app, which parents can use to track their children’s buses. The new version can be downloaded at http://www.petermannbus.com/Pages/PetermannBusTracker.aspx Parents will need their children’s student ID numbers to set up the app. The new app improves functionality from the old version, including giving Petermann Transportation the ability to communicate directly with parents about bus delays and other issues. Parents are reminded that they should not use the system to wait until the very last minute to send their children to the bus stop. Students must be at their designated stop by the scheduled bus arrival time, and waiting until the last minute can de dangerous if children are running to meet their bus. Plus, the system operates by tracking the GPS units in each bus, so it operates on a lag that can be up to two minutes. During that time, the bus may have made progress on its route, so GPS locations are only approximate.

Nurse News about Holiday Stress

Follow the Pickerington School Nurses on Twitter

for school health updates in the district!  @PLSDNurses

 

 

     Ways to Reduce Holiday Stress and Relax this season!

 

 

  1. Put Stress in its Place: Find peace and joy in any season. Focus on the present moment.

 

  1. Create the Holiday you want: Make clear decisions about how you want to spend your time and resources. Consider what is most important to you. What memories will you look back on when the season winds down?

 

  1. Involve your Children: Continue with your child’s regular meal and bedtime schedule. Children feel more secure when their day follows a normal routine. You can offset holiday chaos by involving your children in holiday planning. Having a say in the planning can help your child feel more in control during busy times.

 

  1. Beware of Shopping Pitfalls: People who focus on gifts generally feel less holiday cheer than those who spend more time with close friends and family. Think about what your loved one truly enjoys. Perhaps lunch out together would be appreciated more than a scarf.

 

  1. Get Creative in Your Gift Giving: You don’t have to go into debt to make the holidays special. Have a family meeting and get creative! Drawing names to buy for one person, making homemade gifts or giving a coupon to do the dishes, take the trash out, or take care of pet are ways to save money when gift giving.

 

  1. Play Games: Games can keep things fun and light and a good way to connect with others. Games can be played indoors or out and can be anything from a walk, treasure hunt, board game or cards.

 

  1. Postpone Family Feuds: When you have a group of people in a small area and everyone expecting to have a great time it could be a set up for arguments. With so much emotion and expectation, the holidays are not the best time to work things out. If old family baggage surfaces, plan to talk after the festivities wind down. It’s better to address emotional issues in a more relaxed, private setting.

 

  1. Have Compassion for Yourself and Others: Try not to take things personally. Most people are just trying to get their needs met. Try not to think about how people “should be.” Accept people as they are and where they are coming from. This will show compassion and lessen your frustration.

 

Source: http://www.webmd.com/parenting/family-health-12/reduce-holiday-stress?

Food Pantry Festival of Lights

Our partners at the Pickerington PCMA Food Pantry will kick off their annual major fundraising campaign, “The Festival of Lights,” starting Nov. 15. In 2016, the pantry provided more than 166,623 meals to its clients, all of whom live within Pickerington Local School District.

This year’s theme is “One Light, One Hope.” It is the ninth year the pantry has held the fundraiser. Each holiday lighting bulb that will be used to decorate the pantry’s facility at 70 Cross St. in Olde Pickerington Village represents one $5 gift.

Attendance News

It is hard to believe that we have nearly completed our first trimester of school, Fall is already here and soon the trees will be bare and the cold weather will be upon us. With the onset of cold weather often comes illness and increased absences from school. I would like to take this opportunity to remind parents of the attendance guidelines set forth by the Pickerington School District and the policies specific to Ridgeview.

 

Attendance to school is very important to your child’s academic success. We ask that you help support this standard by encouraging good attendance and making it a priority. Whenever possible, it is preferable that routine appointments are scheduled after school or on days when there is no school.

 

We understand that emergencies and illnesses do occur. In the interest of keeping everyone as healthy as possible, it is advisable that students are kept home when they are ill and not return to school until fever free, without the aid of fever reducing medication, for 24 hours.

In the event that your child is unable to attend school, please notify the school by 8:00 AM. You may call the office, or the Attendance Hotline, (614)- 548-1701, which is available 24 hours, and leave a message. It is always helpful to provide a medical excuse if your child is seen by a physician or medical practitioner. Absences not authorized by a parent will be considered unexcused.

Excessive absences may result in discipline and/or intervention by the District Attendance Officer. Please refer to the PLSD website for a complete explanation of the attendance policies

 

Be on time! Our tardy bell rings at 7:20 AM. Students who are tardy to school or first period class an excessive number of times within the same marking period may be issued an after school two-hour QST (Quiet Study Time) or other appropriate disciplinary consequence.

 

We seek to work with you to ensure your child’s success. If there are extenuating circumstances that contribute to your child’s absences, please feel free to call me to discuss them at any time. Thank you for your support in this matter.

Can My Child Take the ACT or SAT Now?

 

Northwestern University’s Midwest Academic Talent Search (NUMATS) is a research-validated program that utilizes above-grade-level assessment, as a means of gifted testing, to help parents and educators better understand their students’ educational needs. NUMATS allows eligible students to take internationally recognized tests before the grade levels at which they are normally administered. The PSAT™8/9 test, designed for students in grades 8 and 9, is administered to students in grades 3 through 6. SAT® and ACT®, designed for students in grades 11 and 12, are administered to students in grades 6 through 9. NUMATS identifies academic ability, measures growth and connects students to resources and opportunities for advanced students.  You can learn more at

http://www.ctd.northwestern.edu/program/numats

Otis Lennon Testing

On Monday, October 17 the Otis Lennon School Ability Test (OLSAT) will be administered to all seventh grade students in their Social Studies classes. This test is a measurement of abstract thinking and cognitive ability of children. The Otis-Lennon is a group-administered multiple choice test, taken with pencil and paper, measures verbal, quantitative, and spatial reasoning ability. The test yields verbal and nonverbal scores, from which a total score is derived, called a School Ability Index (SAI).

OLSAT measures the cognitive abilities that relate to a student’s ability to learn in school. By assessing a student’s abstract thinking and reasoning abilities, OLSAT supplies educators with information they can use to enhance the insight that traditional achievement tests provide. There is nothing the student needs to do to prepare for this test other than to take the test seriously and to work steadily through it so they can finish this timed test.

WISE Program

With funding and assistance provided by The United Way of Fairfield County,  Ridgeview STEM Jr. High School will again be implementing the WISE program for the 2016/2017 school year.  This year we will have two WISE coaches. One coach who will be staying after school this year to provide extra help.

 

What is WISE?

 

The WISE Program is an educational program designed to improve the organizational skill, study skills, and self confidence of junior high school students.

 

When will the WISE program be implemented?

 

Students can select either first, second or third trimester.  This will take the place of one of their electives.  Students selected to the program will meet 5 days per week during the day.  Students will be withdrawn from one of their electives.

 

Who will be responsible for working with my child during the WISE program periods?

 

The WISE Program will be implemented by a WISE Coach who is a certified classroom teacher, with experience working with students that struggle with staying organized, studying for tests and quizzes, and completing work consistently in a timely manner.

 

Is there data proving the program’s effectiveness?

 

Since the WISE program’s inception in 2008, data has shown that of the 389 students who have participated, 85% have seen an improvement in grades.

 

If you are interested in your child participating in the WISE program, contact your child’s School Counselor at 614-548-1700.  Mrs. Owen works with the 7th grade and Mrs. McGarvey works with the eighth grade.
