The Sciences

“Man is unique not because he does science, and he is unique not because he does art, but because science and art equally are expressions of his marvelous plasticity of mind. “ 
 --Jacob Bronowski, scientist, formerly of the Salk Institute

Scientific literacy has an important role in understand the complex issues of our global society. The rapid pace of technological advances, access to an unprecedented wealth of information, and the pervasive impact of science and technology on day-to-day living require a depth of understanding that can only be enhanced through quality science education. At TAPA, science education focuses on understanding science through the lens scientific method. Students at TAPA do and perform science in order to understand it.

TAPA’s Mission: To produce scientifically literate student artists who possess and understanding of scientific concepts and how they are applied using the performing arts as a vehicle for understanding and application. 

TAPA Vision: To foster a science-art intergraded education by:
  • Providing experiences that excite student’s to learn about how scientific principles are
    applied to using artistic endeavors.
  • Using appropriate scientific processes and principles in making informed decisions.
  • Engaging students intelligently to explore the natural world and technological applications that make life easier.
  • Appling scientific knowledge and skills to their everyday life and chosen careers paths.

TAPA Science Team
Joseph DiStefano, Upper School Science Instructor
Mr.DiStefano@TAPAprovidence.org
Joey DiStefano has a B.S. in Environmental Engineering from Syracuse University's College of Engineering and Computer Science. While at SU, Joey was able to pursue three of his passions: music, engineering, and education. He has played trombone and tuba in multiple musical ensembles including concert bands, pep bands, and Dixieland jazz bands. Over the last four years, Joey has been working on engineering projects around the world. He has spent time in the Middle East with developing water treatment and transport systems as well as spending time in Europe, studying the works of engineering and art of Leonardo da Vinci. Joey loves all outdoor activities, especially hiking, camping, and kayaking. 
Nicole Rollins, Lower School Science Instructor
Ms.Rollins@TAPAprovidence.org
Nicole holds a bachelors degree from the University of Rhode Island in Elementary Education with her Middle School endorsement. She has taught across the state of Rhode Island in multiple classrooms and capacities. Endlessly curious, she is enthusiastic about science and inquiry based instruction. Her artistic passion is dance and outside of school she enjoys sharing this love with her two young daughters.

​
Nick Toscano, Upper School Science Instructor
Mr.Toscano@TAPAprovidence.org
Reflecting his love for both music and science, Nick Toscano holds a Music Degree in Jazz Performance from the University of Hartford as well as a degree in Biological Sciences from the University of Rhode Island. He is certified to teach grades 7-12 Biology and General Science. Nick has worked as an educator of music and science and is an active performing musician. Most recently, Mr. Toscano worked at Sev American College in Istanbul, Turkey teaching high school Biology and coaching the school music ensemble. One of his main focuses was involving his students in intercultural projects and relationships.
