Dance

The dance program at Trinity Academy for the Performing Arts is a comprehensive program which focuses on both dance technique (Ballet, Modern, West-African, Latin, Global Traditions, Hip-Hop, Aesthetics of the Oppressed) as well as using dance (Choreographing, Composing, Devising, and Performing) as a tool for inquiry into contemporary themes and questions that require interdisciplinary knowledge, such as power, oppression, balance, beauty, representation, independence, movement, and transformation.

Dance students will explore creative expression and inquiry through movement; awareness of space, time, and energy as design factors in dance composition; anatomy; and the development of self-confidence through the use of the body as an instrument to negotiate, question, and gain insight. In addition, students will learn the history of multiple forms of dance and formulate their own individual appreciation and philosophy of Dance as a contemporary art form.

Dance Majors and Minors at TAPA will develop kinesthetic awareness, proper body alignment, physical strength, flexibility, endurance, and care for the body. Dance students will work collaboratively to build technique in multiple genres, choreograph/curate original work, and critique professional dance performances. The essential questions and curricular objectives for Dance students across grade levels are supported by the RI Department of Education Grade Span Expectations in Dance (RI GSEs).

TAPA Dance Team
Matt Garza, Dance Artist in Residence
Mr.Garza@TAPAProvidence.org
Matthew Rolando Garza is an educator and performance artist originally from South Texas who has worked in the U.S., Ghana, Mali, South Korea & Costa Rica. A recipient of The Woodrow Wilson-Rockefeller Brothers Fund Fellowship for Aspiring Teachers of Color, M. Garza holds a B.A. in Education History from Brown '11 and an M.A. in Educational Theatre & Social Studies Education from NYU Steinhardt. While in NYC, Garza was a special needs classroom teacher for 3 years, an Art21 Educator, & the Education Director for B&M Fine Art Studios, while he also studied Movement & Ashtanga Yoga with Alvin Ailey & the Joschi Institute. Now a practicing yoga teacher and former Program Director at Breakthrough Providence, a local non-profit organization,  Garza is A 5-year company member of New Works/World Traditions Dance Company  and has been an Artist in Residence at the Yeredon Centre for the Malian Arts in Mali, West Africa.
Brittany Lombardi, Adjunct Dance Artist in Residence
Ms.Lombardi@TAPAProvidence.org
Brittany Lombardi holds a BA from Dean College where she trained in classical ballet, modern (Horton, Limòn, Kline techniques) jazz, hip hop, and tap with renowned artists Jill Silverman, Gregory King, Jean Wenzel, Ricardo Foster, Jamie Jewett, Ali Brodsky, and Jeremy Howes, among others. She currently teaches and choreographs for studios in Rhode Island and Massachusetts in addition to performing with Luminarium Dance Company in Boston. She has danced with Aerplaye Dance Company, and most recently Impact Dance Company.
